# Stem
A new screening test for oral cancer correctly identifies 95 of 100 patients who truly have the
disease, but also incorrectly flags 30 of 100 disease-free patients as positive. This test has
high ______ but relatively low ______.

# Options
- A) sensitivity; specificity (correct)
- B) specificity; sensitivity
- C) positive predictive value; negative predictive value
- D) accuracy; precision

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Sensitivity is the proportion of people WITH the disease who test positive — here 95/100, or 95%,
which is high. Specificity is the proportion of people WITHOUT the disease who test negative —
here only 70/100 correctly test negative (30 false positives), so specificity is a relatively low
70%. This test is good at not missing true cases but tends to over-flag healthy patients.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) This reverses the two terms — the scenario describes correctly catching most true disease
  cases (sensitivity) while incorrectly flagging many disease-free patients (poor specificity),
  the opposite pairing of what is stated here.
- C) Predictive values describe the probability of actually having or not having disease GIVEN a
  positive or negative result, and depend on disease prevalence — a different calculation than
  what is illustrated in this scenario.
- D) "Accuracy" and "precision" are general measurement terms, not the specific sensitivity and
  specificity framework used to evaluate a screening test against known disease status.
