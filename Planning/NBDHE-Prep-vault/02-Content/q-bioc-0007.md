# Stem
A deficiency of which vitamin most directly impairs normal keratinization and differentiation of
oral epithelium, predisposing the tissue to xerostomia and increased susceptibility to infection?

# Options
- A) Vitamin A (correct)
- B) Vitamin B12
- C) Vitamin K
- D) Vitamin E

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Vitamin A (retinol) is required for normal differentiation and maintenance of epithelial and
mucus-secreting tissue. Deficiency causes abnormal keratinization (squamous metaplasia) of
mucosal and glandular epithelium, including salivary gland ducts, which reduces salivary flow and
weakens the epithelial barrier against infection.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Vitamin B12 deficiency produces glossitis and angular cheilitis through impaired cell
  turnover in a megaloblastic process, not through abnormal epithelial keratinization.
- C) Vitamin K is required for synthesis of clotting factors; it has no established role in
  epithelial differentiation.
- D) Vitamin E is a lipid-soluble antioxidant; deficiency is not linked to epithelial
  keratinization defects in the way vitamin A deficiency is.
