# Stem
A patient tells the hygienist, "I know flossing is important, but I have no plans to start doing
it." According to the Transtheoretical (Stages of Change) Model, this patient is in the
precontemplation stage, so individualized instruction at this visit should focus on:

# Options
- A) raising the patient's awareness of the personal risks and benefits of flossing, rather than demonstrating technique (correct)
- B) refining the patient's existing flossing technique through hands-on practice
- C) reinforcing an already-established flossing habit to prevent relapse
- D) helping the patient set a specific start date and providing a technique demonstration

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
The patient acknowledges the behavior's importance but expresses no intention to change — the
definition of precontemplation. Instruction at this stage is most effective when it targets
motivation and personal relevance (why it matters for this patient specifically); skill-building is
more effective once the patient has moved toward the preparation or action stages.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Refining technique assumes the patient is already performing the behavior (the action stage),
  which this patient is not.
- C) Reinforcing a habit applies to the maintenance stage, where a changed behavior is already
  sustained over time.
- D) Setting a start date and demonstrating technique fits the preparation stage, where the patient
  intends to act soon — this patient explicitly has no such plan.
