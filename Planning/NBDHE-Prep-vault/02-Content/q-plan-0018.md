# Stem
When planning oral hygiene instruction for a patient with limited health literacy, the most
effective approach is to:

# Options
- A) provide a detailed pamphlet covering the full biofilm etiology for the patient to study at home
- B) use plain, non-technical language paired with hands-on demonstration and a teach-back check for understanding (correct)
- C) deliver all instruction in a single long, information-dense session to minimize chair time
- D) rely primarily on written post-visit instructions mailed after the appointment

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Individualizing instruction means matching the method to the patient's ability to understand and
use the information. Plain language, hands-on demonstration (e.g., guiding the patient through
brushing technique in the mirror), and teach-back — having the patient show or explain it back —
confirm the instruction actually transferred, which matters most for patients with limited health
literacy.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Dense written material with technical terminology is a poor match for limited health
  literacy and is unlikely to be understood or used.
- C) A single long session risks information overload; instruction is better paced and
  reinforced over time.
- D) Written instructions alone, without demonstration or teach-back, don't confirm
  comprehension and are easily misunderstood by a patient with limited health literacy.
