# Front
How does parathyroid hormone (PTH) raise blood calcium when serum calcium drops, and what are its
three target-organ effects?

# Back
PTH is released by the parathyroid glands in response to low serum calcium. It raises calcium by:
(1) stimulating osteoblasts/stromal cells to upregulate RANKL, which activates **osteoclasts** to
resorb bone and release calcium; (2) increasing **renal tubular reabsorption** of calcium (while
increasing phosphate excretion); (3) activating renal 1α-hydroxylase to produce **active vitamin D**
(1,25-(OH)₂D), which increases intestinal calcium absorption. Calcitonin has the opposite effect —
it *lowers* blood calcium by inhibiting osteoclasts.
