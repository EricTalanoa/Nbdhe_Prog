# Stem
Which white blood cell serves as the primary phagocytic defense against bacteria within the
gingival sulcus?

# Options
- A) neutrophil (polymorphonuclear leukocyte) (correct)
- B) eosinophil
- C) basophil
- D) lymphocyte

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Neutrophils are the first responders in the gingival sulcus, migrating through the junctional
epithelium to phagocytose subgingival plaque bacteria. Neutrophil deficiencies or dysfunction are
linked to severe, early-onset forms of periodontitis.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Eosinophils primarily target parasites and mediate allergic responses, not routine bacterial
  defense in the sulcus.
- C) Basophils release histamine in immediate hypersensitivity reactions and play only a minor
  role in periodontal defense.
- D) Lymphocytes drive the adaptive immune response in chronic periodontal lesions but are not the
  primary first-line phagocyte.
