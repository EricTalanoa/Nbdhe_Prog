# Stem
Enamel is formed during tooth development by which cells?

# Options
- A) odontoblasts
- B) ameloblasts (correct)
- C) cementoblasts
- D) fibroblasts

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Ameloblasts, derived from the inner enamel epithelium (ectoderm), secrete the enamel matrix during
amelogenesis. They are lost once enamel formation is complete, which is why mature enamel cannot
regenerate itself.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Odontoblasts form dentin and remain able to produce reparative dentin.
- C) Cementoblasts form cementum on the root surface.
- D) Fibroblasts produce collagen for the periodontal ligament and connective tissue, not enamel.
