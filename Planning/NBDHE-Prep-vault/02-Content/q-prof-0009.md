# Stem
A cognitively intact adult patient, after being informed of the risks of declining periodontal
treatment, still refuses the recommended scaling and root planing. The hygienist documents the
discussion and respects the patient's decision. This action primarily reflects which ethical
principle?

# Options
- A) Beneficence
- B) Autonomy (correct)
- C) Nonmaleficence
- D) Justice

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Autonomy obligates providers to respect a competent, adequately informed patient's right to make
their own healthcare decisions, even when that decision differs from the clinical recommendation.
Documenting the discussion protects both the patient's autonomy and the provider.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Beneficence is the duty to act in ways that benefit the patient — it is actually in tension
  with, not the reason for, honoring a refusal of that same recommended treatment.
- C) Nonmaleficence ("do no harm") is more directly implicated when weighing a risk of harm from a
  procedure; it does not primarily explain why the patient's own decision is respected here.
- D) Justice concerns fairness in access to and distribution of care across patients, unrelated to
  an individual patient's right to accept or decline their own treatment.
