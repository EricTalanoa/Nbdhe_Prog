# Stem
For a patient with significant cardiovascular disease, the epinephrine dose in a local anesthetic
is limited primarily because epinephrine can:

# Options
- A) increase heart rate and myocardial oxygen demand (correct)
- B) provoke an immediate IgE-mediated allergic reaction
- C) prolong bleeding at the injection site
- D) make the solution more alkaline and dangerously speed onset

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Epinephrine is a sympathomimetic: it raises heart rate, contractility, and blood pressure, which
increases myocardial oxygen demand. In a cardiovascularly compromised patient this added cardiac
stress is the concern, so the dose is held to a conservative "cardiac dose" (about 0.04 mg per
appointment) with careful aspiration.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Epinephrine is identical to an endogenous hormone and does not cause true allergy; the
  sulfite antioxidant added to vasoconstrictor solutions is the more relevant sensitivity concern.
- C) Vasoconstriction reduces bleeding in the field — the opposite effect.
- D) Vasoconstrictor-containing solutions are actually more acidic, and pH is not the reason to
  limit epinephrine in cardiac patients.
