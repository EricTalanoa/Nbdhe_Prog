# Front
What single behavior defines a neoplasm as malignant rather than benign, and what does a benign
tumor do instead?

# Back
**Malignant** = capable of **invasion and metastasis** — it breaks through basement membranes,
infiltrates adjacent tissue, and spreads to distant sites via lymph or blood. **Benign** tumors
stay localized, are usually **encapsulated**, and grow by pushing tissue aside. Growth rate, size,
and cell of origin do *not* define malignancy — invasion/metastasis does.
