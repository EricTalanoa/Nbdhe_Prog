# Stem
Under the Spaulding classification system, a mouth mirror that will contact oral mucosa but will
not penetrate soft tissue or bone is categorized as:

# Options
- A) critical, requiring disposal after a single use
- B) semicritical, requiring at minimum high-level disinfection, with heat sterilization preferred (correct)
- C) noncritical, requiring only intermediate-level surface wiping
- D) critical, requiring only a chemical indicator strip to confirm processing

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
The Spaulding system sorts instruments by infection risk. Semicritical items contact mucous
membranes or nonintact skin but don't penetrate tissue, so they need at least high-level
disinfection; because most dental semicritical instruments can withstand heat, CDC guidance
prefers steam sterilization for them over disinfection alone.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Critical instruments are the ones that penetrate soft tissue or bone (e.g., scalers used
  subgingivally) and must be sterilized, not merely disposed of — and disposal isn't the defining
  requirement of the critical category anyway.
- C) Noncritical items only contact intact skin (e.g., a blood pressure cuff); a mirror touching
  mucosa is semicritical, not noncritical.
- D) A chemical indicator only shows exposure to a process, not that sterilization or high-level
  disinfection was actually achieved.
