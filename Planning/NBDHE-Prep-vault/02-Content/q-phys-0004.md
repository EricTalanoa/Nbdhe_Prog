# Stem
When blood pressure suddenly rises, the baroreceptor reflex acts to ______ heart rate in order to
help return blood pressure back toward normal.

# Options
- A) increase
- B) decrease (correct)
- C) not affect
- D) first increase, then decrease

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Baroreceptors in the carotid sinus and aortic arch sense the increased stretch from higher
pressure and increase their afferent firing rate to the vasomotor center. This raises
parasympathetic (vagal) output and lowers sympathetic output to the heart, slowing heart rate and
reducing cardiac output — a negative-feedback response that helps bring blood pressure back down.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) This is the intuitive but backward answer — the reflex works as negative feedback, opposing
  the rise in pressure, not amplifying it by speeding up the heart further.
- C) The baroreceptor reflex is a fast-acting, moment-to-moment regulator; it does actively adjust
  heart rate in response to acute pressure changes.
- D) The reflex response is a sustained decrease for as long as pressure remains elevated, not a
  brief increase followed by a decrease.
