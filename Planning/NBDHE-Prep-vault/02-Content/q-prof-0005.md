# Stem
A dental hygienist notices that a colleague is consistently over-scheduling appointments so that
scaling and root planing is rushed and incomplete. Which ethical principle most directly obligates
the hygienist to address this?

# Options
- A) Nonmaleficence — the duty to avoid causing harm to patients (correct)
- B) Autonomy — the duty to respect a patient's right to self-determination
- C) Justice — the duty to distribute resources and treatment fairly
- D) Confidentiality — the duty to protect private patient information

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Nonmaleficence ("do no harm") obligates clinicians to avoid practices that place patients at risk
of harm — here, rushed, incomplete periodontal therapy risks disease progression. This principle
supports raising the concern through appropriate channels (e.g., practice management, licensing
board if unresolved).

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Autonomy concerns a patient's right to make informed choices about their own care, not the
  quality/thoroughness of the care being rendered.
- C) Justice concerns fair distribution of care/resources across patients, not the completeness of
  one procedure.
- D) Confidentiality concerns protecting patient information, unrelated to rushed treatment.
