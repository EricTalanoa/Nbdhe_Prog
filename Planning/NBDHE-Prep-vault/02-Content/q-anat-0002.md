# Stem
Which permanent tooth most commonly has two roots — one buccal and one palatal?

# Options
- A) maxillary first premolar (correct)
- B) mandibular first premolar
- C) maxillary canine
- D) mandibular first molar

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
The maxillary first premolar most often has two roots — a buccal and a palatal (lingual) root —
which is clinically relevant during extraction and instrumentation.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) The mandibular first premolar is typically single-rooted.
- C) The maxillary canine has a single, very long root.
- D) The mandibular first molar usually has two roots, but they are mesial and distal, not buccal
  and palatal.
