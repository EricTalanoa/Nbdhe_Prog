# Stem
When an error is made in a paper dental record, the correct way to handle it is to:

# Options
- A) cover the entry with correction fluid and write over it
- B) draw a single dated, initialed line through the error, leaving the original legible (correct)
- C) erase the entry so the chart looks clean
- D) remove and rewrite the entire page

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Records must be accurate, objective, and contemporaneous. A correction is made with a single line
through the error that is dated and initialed and that keeps the original entry readable. This
preserves the integrity and legal defensibility of the record.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Correction fluid obscures the original and looks like tampering.
- C) Erasing destroys the original entry and undermines the record's credibility.
- D) Removing or rewriting pages destroys the contemporaneous record and raises suspicion of
  alteration.
