# Stem
The ALARA principle in dental radiography directs the clinician to:

# Options
- A) expose radiographs at fixed intervals regardless of clinical need
- B) keep patient radiation exposure As Low As Reasonably Achievable (correct)
- C) use the highest available kilovoltage to shorten exposure time
- D) routinely retake films to guarantee diagnostic quality

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
ALARA stands for "As Low As Reasonably Achievable." It is applied through fast image receptors,
rectangular collimation, appropriate patient shielding, sound technique to avoid retakes, and
prescribing radiographs based on the individual patient's needs.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Radiographs are prescribed on the basis of clinical need, not fixed time intervals.
- C) Changing kilovoltage affects contrast; the ALARA aim is dose minimization, not speed at any cost.
- D) Routine retakes increase, rather than reduce, patient exposure.
