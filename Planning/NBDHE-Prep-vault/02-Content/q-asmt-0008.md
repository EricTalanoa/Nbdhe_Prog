# Stem
Overjet is best described as the:

# Options
- A) vertical overlap of the maxillary anterior teeth over the mandibular anterior teeth
- B) horizontal distance between the labial surfaces of the mandibular and maxillary incisors (correct)
- C) mesiodistal rotation of a tooth within its socket
- D) discrepancy between centric relation and centric occlusion

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Overjet is a horizontal measurement: the distance the maxillary incisal edges project facially
beyond the labial surfaces of the mandibular incisors when the teeth are in occlusion.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) That vertical overlap describes overbite, not overjet.
- C) Mesiodistal rotation describes a tooth-position anomaly, not a measurement between the arches.
- D) A discrepancy between centric relation and centric occlusion describes a slide/shift in
  mandibular position, not a horizontal incisor measurement.
