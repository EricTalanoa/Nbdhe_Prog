# Stem
Air polishing with a traditional sodium bicarbonate powder is specifically contraindicated for a
patient who:

# Options
- A) is on a sodium-restricted diet (correct)
- B) has moderate extrinsic tobacco stain
- C) has a full gold crown on a molar
- D) drinks coffee and tea daily

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Sodium bicarbonate air-polishing powder delivers appreciable sodium, so it is contraindicated for
patients on sodium-restricted diets (and used with caution in respiratory disease or uncontrolled
hypertension). A low-abrasive alternative such as glycine powder can be substituted.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Extrinsic stain is an indication for air polishing, not a contraindication.
- C) Gold restorations tolerate air polishing; the concern here is the sodium load, not the crown.
- D) Coffee and tea produce extrinsic stain — again an indication for stain removal.
