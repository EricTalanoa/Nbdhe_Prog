# Stem
Using the 2017 ACC/AHA classification, an adult blood pressure of 135/85 mmHg is classified as:

# Options
- A) normal
- B) elevated
- C) stage 1 hypertension (correct)
- D) stage 2 hypertension

# Correct answer
C

# Rationale
Under the 2017 ACC/AHA categories, a systolic of 130–139 mmHg OR a diastolic of 80–89 mmHg is
Stage 1 hypertension. A reading of 135/85 falls in that band and warrants monitoring and medical
referral as indicated.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Normal is less than 120 systolic AND less than 80 diastolic.
- B) Elevated is 120–129 systolic AND less than 80 diastolic; the diastolic of 85 rules this out.
- D) Stage 2 hypertension is 140 or higher systolic OR 90 or higher diastolic.
