# Stem
A locally delivered antimicrobial agent placed subgingivally as an adjunct to scaling and root
planing is most appropriately indicated for:

# Options
- A) generalized gingivitis with no attachment loss
- B) an isolated periodontal pocket that has not responded adequately to mechanical therapy alone (correct)
- C) full-mouth disinfection in a patient with generalized severe periodontitis
- D) routine maintenance visits with no active disease present

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Locally delivered antimicrobials are indicated as a site-specific adjunct for isolated, persistent
pockets (commonly ≥5 mm with continued inflammation) that have not fully resolved after
conventional scaling and root planing, delivering a sustained, high local drug concentration
directly into the pocket.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Gingivitis without attachment loss resolves with improved biofilm control and mechanical
  debridement; a subgingival antimicrobial is not indicated.
- C) Full-mouth generalized disease is managed with systemic/mechanical strategies; localized
  agents are for isolated refractory sites, not a full-mouth approach.
- D) With no active disease present, there is no indication for placing an antimicrobial agent.
