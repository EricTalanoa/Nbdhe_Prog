# Stem
Which cell is primarily responsible for the resorption of alveolar bone during periodontal disease?

# Options
- A) osteoblast
- B) osteoclast (correct)
- C) osteocyte
- D) fibroblast

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Osteoclasts are large multinucleated cells that dissolve the mineral phase and digest the organic
matrix of bone, releasing calcium into the circulation. In periodontitis, host inflammatory
mediators (RANKL, prostaglandins, and cytokines) drive osteoclast activity, tipping the normal
remodeling balance toward net bone loss around the tooth.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Osteoblasts **build** bone by secreting osteoid that later mineralizes — the opposite of
  resorption. Their relative underactivity contributes to net loss, but they do not remove bone.
- C) Osteocytes are mature osteoblasts trapped within the mineralized matrix; they act as
  mechanosensors that regulate remodeling but do not themselves resorb bone.
- D) Fibroblasts produce the collagen and ground substance of soft connective tissue (such as the
  periodontal ligament and gingiva); they are not bone-resorbing cells.
