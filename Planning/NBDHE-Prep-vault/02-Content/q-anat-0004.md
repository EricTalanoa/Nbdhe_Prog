# Stem
What term describes the line marking the junction between the enamel of the crown and the
cementum of the root?

# Options
- A) cementoenamel junction (CEJ) (correct)
- B) height of contour
- C) line angle
- D) mamelon

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
The cementoenamel junction (CEJ), also called the cervical line, is the anatomic boundary where
the enamel covering the crown meets the cementum covering the root.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Height of contour is the greatest mesiodistal or facio-lingual bulge of the crown, unrelated
  to the crown-root boundary.
- C) A line angle is formed where two tooth surfaces meet (e.g., mesiofacial), not the crown-root
  junction.
- D) Mamelons are the three rounded protuberances on the incisal edge of a newly erupted incisor,
  unrelated to the CEJ.
