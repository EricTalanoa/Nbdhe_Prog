# Stem
The rapid depolarization phase of a neuronal action potential is caused primarily by the influx of
which ion?

# Options
- A) potassium
- B) calcium
- C) sodium (correct)
- D) chloride

# Correct answer
C

# Rationale
When threshold is reached, voltage-gated sodium channels open and Na+ rushes into the cell down its
electrochemical gradient, producing the rapid upstroke (depolarization). This is precisely the
mechanism local anesthetics interrupt by blocking those sodium channels.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Potassium efflux drives repolarization, returning the membrane toward its resting potential.
- B) Calcium influx is central to synaptic transmission and cardiac cells but is not the main driver
  of the nerve action potential upstroke.
- D) Chloride movement is generally inhibitory (hyperpolarizing), not depolarizing.
