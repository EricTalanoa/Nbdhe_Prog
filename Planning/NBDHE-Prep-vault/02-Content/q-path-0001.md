# Stem
The most common intraoral site for squamous cell carcinoma is the:

# Options
- A) hard palate
- B) dorsum of the tongue
- C) posterior-lateral border and ventral surface of the tongue (correct)
- D) attached gingiva

# Correct answer
C

# Rationale
Oral squamous cell carcinoma most commonly arises on the posterior-lateral and ventral surfaces of
the tongue and the floor of the mouth — "gutter" areas where salivary-dissolved carcinogens pool.
These sites warrant careful examination and palpation.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Hard palate SCC is uncommon; palatal masses are more often of salivary-gland origin.
- B) The dorsum of the tongue is an uncommon site for SCC.
- D) Attached gingiva is a far less common location than the lateral tongue and floor of the mouth.
