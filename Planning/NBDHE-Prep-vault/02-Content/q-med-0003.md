# Stem
The relationship between diabetes mellitus and periodontal disease is best described as:

# Options
- A) bidirectional — poorly controlled diabetes worsens periodontal disease severity, and periodontal infection can worsen glycemic control (correct)
- B) unrelated — diabetes has no established effect on periodontal status
- C) protective — hyperglycemia reduces the inflammatory response and lowers periodontal disease risk
- D) unidirectional — periodontal disease affects blood glucose, but diabetes has no effect on periodontal tissues

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Extensive evidence supports a bidirectional relationship: poorly controlled diabetes impairs
neutrophil function and collagen metabolism, worsening periodontal destruction, while periodontal
inflammation raises systemic inflammatory mediators that reduce insulin sensitivity and complicate
glycemic control.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Diabetes is a well-established major risk factor for periodontal disease, so the two are not
  unrelated.
- C) Hyperglycemia impairs immune and healing responses, increasing — not decreasing —
  susceptibility to periodontal breakdown.
- D) Research shows diabetes also affects periodontal tissues, not only the reverse, so the
  relationship runs both ways rather than one way.
