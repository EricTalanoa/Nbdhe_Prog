# Stem
A patient presents with acute pain from a symptomatic carious lesion along with generalized
moderate chronic periodontitis requiring nonsurgical therapy. All of the following are appropriate
approaches to sequencing this patient's care EXCEPT:

# Options
- A) addressing the acute, pain-producing problem promptly, even before completing the systematic disease-control phase
- B) individualizing the sequence to this patient's clinical needs and priorities
- C) treating the standard phase order as fixed and non-negotiable, and deferring the chief complaint until it is reached (correct)
- D) coordinating timing with the dentist if the acute problem requires restorative or endodontic intervention

# Correct answer
C

# Rationale
Good treatment-plan sequencing adapts a systematic framework (disease control, then corrective/
restorative, then maintenance) to the individual patient. An acute, symptomatic problem is
addressed promptly rather than deferred to preserve a rigid, fixed order — treating the phase order
as absolute and ignoring the chief complaint is not appropriate sequencing.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Prioritizing prompt relief of a symptomatic, pain-producing problem is standard, appropriate
  practice.
- B) Individualizing the sequence to the patient's condition and priorities is a core principle of
  treatment planning.
- D) Coordinating with the dentist for care beyond the hygienist's scope (e.g., restorative or
  endodontic treatment) is appropriate and expected.
