# Stem
During the disease-control phase of therapy, a periodontal re-evaluation appointment is scheduled
primarily to:

# Options
- A) begin definitive restorative treatment immediately
- B) assess tissue response to initial therapy and determine whether further treatment, such as surgery, is indicated (correct)
- C) discharge the patient to a routine maintenance interval regardless of healing
- D) obtain the patient's initial informed consent for the original treatment plan

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Re-evaluation, typically 4-6 weeks after nonsurgical therapy, measures how tissue responded —
reduced inflammation, probing depths, bleeding on probing — so the plan can be modified: some
sites may need only maintenance, while others with persistent pockets may require referral for
surgical therapy. It's a decision point, not an automatic step to the next phase.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Definitive restorative work follows disease control and re-evaluation, not the reverse.
- C) Placing a patient on maintenance without evaluating healing skips the assessment the
  appointment exists to perform.
- D) Informed consent is obtained before treatment begins during case presentation, not at the
  post-therapy re-evaluation.
