# Stem
A young child ingests a potentially toxic quantity of fluoride. Along with activating emergency
care, an appropriate immediate first-aid measure is to have the child drink:

# Options
- A) milk or another calcium-containing liquid (correct)
- B) a large glass of plain water only
- C) a carbonated soft drink
- D) a citrus fruit juice

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Calcium binds fluoride in the gastrointestinal tract and slows its absorption, so milk (or another
calcium source) is given for fluoride ingestion while emergency care is arranged based on the dose
taken.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Water may dilute but does not bind fluoride; calcium-containing liquids are preferred.
- C) A soft drink provides no calcium binding and its acidity is unhelpful.
- D) Fruit juice does not bind fluoride and adds acid.
