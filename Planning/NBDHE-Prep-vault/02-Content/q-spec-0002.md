# Stem
Given this patient's sensory aversion to bristle texture and mint flavor, which home-care
recommendation is most appropriate for the caregiver?

# Options
- A) switch to a soft-bristled or vibrating power toothbrush and an unflavored or mild-flavor fluoride toothpaste, keeping the same brushing routine and order each time (correct)
- B) substitute a non-fluoride toothpaste so taste sensitivity is no longer a concern
- C) reduce brushing to once every few days until the patient tolerates it better on his own
- D) have the patient brush independently without caregiver assistance so he controls the sensory input

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Modifying the sensory inputs the patient is reacting to — bristle texture/vibration and taste —
while keeping the caregiver-assisted routine and sequence consistent addresses the specific
aversions without removing needed daily plaque control or fluoride exposure. A predictable order
of steps also reduces anxiety, reinforcing the desensitization approach used chairside.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Fluoride remains the standard caries-preventive agent for this patient; taste sensitivity
  should be managed by choosing a mild or unflavored fluoride product, not by removing fluoride.
- C) Reducing brushing frequency allows plaque and gingival inflammation to worsen and does not
  address the underlying sensory aversion.
- D) Given his level of support needs and difficulty completing brushing even with assistance,
  removing caregiver assistance is likely to result in incomplete plaque removal rather than
  improved tolerance.
