# Stem
When a dental hygienist refers a patient to a periodontist for surgical evaluation, which
communication practice best supports continuity of care?

# Options
- A) sending a written referral summarizing findings, treatment rendered, and the specific reason for referral (correct)
- B) telling the patient to describe their own history to the specialist from memory
- C) relying on the patient's next general-office visit to relay the referral verbally
- D) sending only the patient's contact information with no clinical summary

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
A written (or securely transmitted) referral summarizing periodontal findings, treatment already
rendered, and the specific reason for referral gives the specialist the clinical context needed for
an efficient, accurate evaluation and supports coordinated, continuous care between providers.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Patients often omit or misremember clinical details; relying solely on patient recall risks
  incomplete or inaccurate information reaching the specialist.
- C) Delaying the referral information to a future visit does not support timely, coordinated care.
- D) Contact information alone gives the specialist no clinical context to prepare for the visit.
