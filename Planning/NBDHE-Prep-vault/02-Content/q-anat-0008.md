# Stem
Which muscle of mastication is primarily responsible for protruding the mandible?

# Options
- A) masseter
- B) temporalis
- C) medial pterygoid
- D) lateral pterygoid (correct)

# Correct answer
D

# Rationale
The lateral pterygoid's inferior head pulls the condyle and mandible forward, producing protrusion
(and, acting unilaterally, contributing to lateral excursion to the opposite side).

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) The masseter is a powerful elevator of the mandible (closes the jaw), not a protrusor.
- B) The temporalis primarily elevates the mandible, and its posterior fibers retrude it.
- C) The medial pterygoid mainly elevates the mandible and assists in protrusion, but the lateral
  pterygoid is the principal, defining protrusor.
