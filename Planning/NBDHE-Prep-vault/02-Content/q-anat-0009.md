# Stem
Which anatomic landmark is the actual opening through which the inferior alveolar nerve and
vessels enter the mandibular canal?

# Options
- A) Mandibular foramen (correct)
- B) Lingula
- C) Mental foramen
- D) Mylohyoid groove

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
The mandibular foramen, on the medial surface of the ramus, is the actual opening through which
the inferior alveolar nerve and vessels enter the mandibular canal — the target of an inferior
alveolar nerve block.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) The lingula is the bony spine immediately anterior and superior to the mandibular foramen
  (an attachment point for the sphenomandibular ligament); it is a palpable landmark used to help
  locate the foramen during an injection, but it is not the opening itself.
- C) The mental foramen is where a terminal branch of the inferior alveolar nerve (the mental
  nerve) exits the mandible near the second premolar apex — an exit point, not the entry point
  into the canal, despite the similar-sounding name.
- D) The mylohyoid groove carries the mylohyoid nerve and vessels along the medial mandible near
  the foramen, but it is a groove for an accessory branch, not the canal's main entry point.
