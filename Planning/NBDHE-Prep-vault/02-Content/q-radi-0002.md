# Stem
A bitewing radiograph is the projection of choice for detecting:

# Options
- A) periapical pathology at the root apex
- B) interproximal caries and early crestal bone loss (correct)
- C) the position of impacted third molars
- D) temporomandibular joint disorders

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
A bitewing captures the crowns and crestal bone of both arches in a single image, making it ideal
for detecting interproximal (proximal) caries and evaluating early alveolar crest bone levels.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Periapical pathology requires a periapical radiograph that includes the root apex.
- C) Impacted third molars are best evaluated on a panoramic or periapical image.
- D) TMJ disorders require dedicated imaging such as panoramic, CBCT, or MRI.
