# Stem
The "red complex" bacteria most strongly associated with periodontal pocket depth and bleeding on
probing include Porphyromonas gingivalis, Tannerella forsythia, and:

# Options
- A) Treponema denticola (correct)
- B) Streptococcus mutans
- C) Actinomyces viscosus
- D) Fusobacterium nucleatum

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Socransky's red complex — P. gingivalis, T. forsythia, and T. denticola — is the microbial cluster
most consistently linked to established periodontitis, deep pocketing, and bleeding on probing.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Streptococcus mutans is a primary cariogenic organism, not a member of the periodontal red
  complex.
- C) Actinomyces viscosus is an early biofilm colonizer associated with root-surface caries, not
  the red complex.
- D) Fusobacterium nucleatum belongs to the orange complex; it bridges early and late colonizers
  but is not one of the three red-complex species.
