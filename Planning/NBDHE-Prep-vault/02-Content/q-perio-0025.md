# Stem
In the current periodontitis classification system, the descriptor that reflects a patient's risk
for future disease PROGRESSION (based on factors such as smoking status, diabetes control, and the
ratio of destruction to local factors) is the periodontitis ______.

# Options
- A) Stage
- B) Grade (correct)
- C) Extent
- D) Type

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Stage (I–IV) reflects the severity of destruction already present — interdental CAL, radiographic
bone loss, and tooth loss due to periodontitis. Grade (A–C) reflects the rate of progression and
future risk, informed by direct evidence (prior bone loss over time) or indirect risk indicators
like smoking and glycemic control.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Stage describes the severity of damage already present, not the risk of future progression —
  the point of confusion this item tests.
- C) Extent (localized, generalized, or molar-incisor pattern) is a modifier describing how much
  of the dentition is affected, not a progression-risk descriptor.
- D) "Type" is not a descriptor used in the current classification framework.
