# Stem
When evaluating periodontal health at a maintenance visit, the absence of bleeding on probing
(BOP-negative) at a site is most useful clinically because it:

# Options
- A) has a high negative predictive value, meaning a BOP-negative site is very unlikely to be undergoing active attachment loss (correct)
- B) confirms that the site has never had prior attachment loss or periodontal disease
- C) is a more sensitive single indicator of disease activity than probing depth or clinical attachment level combined
- D) guarantees that subgingival calculus is absent at that site

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Bleeding on probing has been shown to have high negative predictive value for periodontal
stability: a site that does not bleed on gentle probing is very unlikely to be losing attachment,
making repeated BOP-negative findings over successive maintenance visits a reassuring sign of
stability. Its positive predictive value is lower, however, since a bleeding site does not always
mean disease is progressing (transient trauma, technique, or timing can cause a false positive).

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) A stable, BOP-negative site can still have a history of prior attachment loss (e.g., a
  successfully treated site with recession); absence of bleeding reflects current inflammatory
  status, not treatment history.
- C) No single clinical parameter is fully sensitive alone; BOP is most useful in combination with
  probing depth and clinical attachment level, not as a replacement for them.
- D) Bleeding on probing reflects gingival inflammatory status, not directly the presence or absence
  of subgingival calculus, which requires tactile exploration to detect.
