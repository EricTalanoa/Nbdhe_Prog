# Demographics
Female, age 78. Hypertension managed with hydrochlorothiazide, mild depression managed with
amitriptyline, and osteoarthritis affecting both hands. No known drug allergies. Lives
independently.

# Chief Complaint
"My mouth feels so dry all the time, and I've had three cavities this year — I never used to get
any."

# Background / History
No new caries in over 20 years until the past year. Reports sipping sweetened tea and sucking on
hard candy throughout the day to relieve the dry-mouth feeling. Osteoarthritis in both hands has
made brushing more difficult and less thorough than it used to be. Wears a mandibular removable
partial denture that fits loosely.

# Current Findings
Visibly diminished resting salivary flow with thick, ropey saliva. New cervical/root-surface
carious lesions on the mandibular anterior and premolar teeth, an area with generalized moderate
gingival recession exposing root surfaces. Moderate plaque accumulation consistent with reduced
manual dexterity. No acute swelling or pain reported.
