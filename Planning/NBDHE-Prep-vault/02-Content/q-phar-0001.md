# Stem
Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) such as ibuprofen relieve pain and inflammation mainly
by inhibiting which enzyme?

# Options
- A) cyclooxygenase (COX) (correct)
- B) lipoxygenase
- C) phospholipase A2
- D) acetylcholinesterase

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
NSAIDs inhibit cyclooxygenase (COX-1 and COX-2), reducing the synthesis of prostaglandins that
mediate pain, inflammation, and fever.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Lipoxygenase produces leukotrienes and is not the NSAID target.
- C) Phospholipase A2 is inhibited upstream by corticosteroids, not by NSAIDs.
- D) Acetylcholinesterase breaks down acetylcholine and is unrelated to NSAID action.
