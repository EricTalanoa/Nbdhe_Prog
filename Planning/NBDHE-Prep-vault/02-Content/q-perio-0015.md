# Stem
The primary purpose of placing a periodontal dressing (pack) immediately after periodontal surgery
is to:

# Options
- A) protect the surgical site, stabilize mobile tissue and any graft material, and improve patient comfort during initial healing (correct)
- B) deliver a sustained-release systemic antibiotic to the surgical site
- C) permanently splint the adjacent teeth together
- D) whiten the surgical site to mask postoperative bleeding

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
A periodontal dressing is a temporary, inert cover placed over the surgical wound. It protects the
healing tissue from mechanical trauma and oral fluids, helps hold flaps and any graft material in
position, and reduces postoperative discomfort during the first week of healing.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Standard periodontal dressings are not drug-delivery devices; they contain no systemic
  antibiotic.
- C) Dressings are removed within about a week and are not a permanent splinting solution;
  long-term splinting uses separate fixed appliances.
- D) Dressings are protective, not cosmetic, and do not function to mask bleeding.
