# Stem
A patient with a history of asthma becomes acutely short of breath and audibly wheezing during
treatment. The most appropriate immediate management is to:

# Options
- A) position the patient upright and administer their short-acting bronchodilator inhaler (correct)
- B) lay the patient supine and elevate the legs
- C) give sublingual nitroglycerin
- D) begin rescue breathing immediately

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
An acute asthma attack is treated by sitting the patient upright (which eases breathing),
administering their short-acting beta-2 agonist (e.g., albuterol), giving oxygen, and monitoring.
Most attacks respond to the bronchodilator.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) A supine position with legs raised is management for syncope; it makes breathing harder in
  respiratory distress.
- C) Sublingual nitroglycerin is for anginal chest pain, not bronchospasm.
- D) The patient is breathing (wheezing implies airflow); rescue breathing is for a patient who is
  not breathing.
