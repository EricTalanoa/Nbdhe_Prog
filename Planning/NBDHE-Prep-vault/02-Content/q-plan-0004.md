# Stem
When teaching a newly diagnosed gingivitis patient why daily plaque removal is the foundation of
treatment, the most effective explanation is that:

# Options
- A) gingivitis is caused by bacterial biofilm at the gingival margin, and disrupting it daily resolves the inflammation (correct)
- B) gingivitis is a hereditary condition that home care cannot influence
- C) professional scaling alone, without any daily home care, will keep gingivitis from recurring
- D) mouthwash use can fully replace mechanical plaque removal

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Gingivitis is a reversible, biofilm-induced inflammatory response. Individualized education should
tie the etiology (bacterial plaque) directly to the recommended behavior (consistent daily
mechanical removal), which is what actually resolves the inflammation and is the core message of
periodontal patient education.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Plaque-induced gingivitis is primarily a response to local bacterial biofilm, not a purely
  hereditary condition, and it responds well to improved home care.
- C) Without daily home care, biofilm reforms within hours to days and inflammation recurs; a
  single professional visit is not sufficient on its own.
- D) Rinses can be a helpful adjunct but do not mechanically disrupt biofilm the way brushing and
  interdental cleaning do, so they cannot fully replace mechanical removal.
