# Stem
A 9-year-old patient presents with several new carious lesions on the occlusal surfaces of
recently erupted permanent molars. Which of the following is the most appropriate first step in
individualized patient education for caries control?

# Options
- A) discuss the frequency of fermentable-carbohydrate exposure and demonstrate effective toothbrushing technique with a fluoride toothpaste (correct)
- B) tell the caregiver to schedule stainless steel crowns on all affected teeth
- C) advise the family to discontinue fluoride toothpaste until the lesions are restored
- D) instruct the caregiver to apply silver diamine fluoride to the lesions at home

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Dental caries results from the interaction of fermentable carbohydrates, cariogenic biofilm, and
host factors over time. Individualized education that targets modifiable behaviors — sugar
exposure frequency and effective fluoride-toothpaste brushing — is the hygienist's first-line
preventive contribution, alongside referral for any needed restorative care.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Deciding on and placing restorations is a treatment-planning/restorative decision for the
  dentist, not a patient-education step.
- C) Fluoride reduces caries risk; discontinuing it would work against the treatment goal.
- D) Silver diamine fluoride is a professionally applied topical agent, not a product intended for
  caregiver self-application at home.
