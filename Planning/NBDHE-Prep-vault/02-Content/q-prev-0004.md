# Stem
Professionally applied 5% sodium fluoride varnish is best classified as a:

# Options
- A) topical fluoride applied in the office (correct)
- B) systemic fluoride supplement
- C) form of community water fluoridation
- D) low-concentration daily home rinse

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Fluoride varnish (5% NaF, ~22,600 ppm F) is a professionally applied topical agent that adheres to
the enamel and releases fluoride over several hours, promoting remineralization. It acts locally,
not systemically.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Systemic fluorides are ingested supplements (tablets/drops) prescribed by caries risk and
  water fluoride level.
- C) Community water fluoridation is a systemic public-health measure delivered through drinking
  water.
- D) Home fluoride rinses are self-applied and far lower in concentration than varnish.
