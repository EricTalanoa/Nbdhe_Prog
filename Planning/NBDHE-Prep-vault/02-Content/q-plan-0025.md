# Stem
A patient presents with an acute periodontal abscess causing pain and swelling, generalized plaque
accumulation, and a stated desire to learn better flossing technique. Using the human needs
conceptual model to prioritize the resulting dental hygiene diagnoses, the hygienist should address
first the unmet need for:

# Options
- A) freedom from pain and stress, since the acute abscess represents the most urgent physiologic and safety threat (correct)
- B) skin and mucous membrane integrity, because plaque accumulation is the underlying etiology of the abscess
- C) conceptualization and problem solving, because flossing instruction is the patient's stated priority
- D) responsibility for oral health, because long-term self-care ultimately prevents recurrence

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
The human needs conceptual model, like Maslow's hierarchy it draws on, prioritizes physiologic and
safety needs above learning or self-care needs. An acute abscess causing pain requires the most
urgent intervention (freedom from pain and stress) before addressing contributing etiology or
patient education, even though all three needs are legitimately unmet and will eventually be
addressed in the comprehensive care plan.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Skin and mucous membrane integrity (the plaque-related etiology) matters, but it is a
  longer-term contributing factor, not the immediate threat the abscess poses.
- C) The patient's stated interest in flossing technique is valuable, but a stated preference does
  not override the more urgent, higher-priority physiologic need.
- D) Responsibility for oral health is a valid long-term goal, but it is not the priority need when
  an acute, painful condition is present and unaddressed.
