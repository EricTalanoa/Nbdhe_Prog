# Demographics
Male, age 24. Diagnosed with autism spectrum disorder (ASD); nonverbal, communicates through a
picture-based augmentative communication system. Pronounced sensory sensitivity to touch, taste,
and unfamiliar sounds. No known drug allergies. Accompanied to the appointment by his mother, who
is his primary caregiver.

# Chief Complaint
Caregiver reports (patient is nonverbal): "He fights us every time we try to brush his teeth at
home, so I know his gums probably aren't in great shape. I want him to actually get through a
cleaning without a meltdown."

# Background / History
Caregiver-assisted toothbrushing twice daily, but sessions are frequently cut short because the
patient resists the bristle texture and mint flavor. No prior general anesthesia for dental care.
Tolerated his last two recall visits reasonably well after the office began using a consistent
pre-visit routine. No significant medical history beyond ASD; takes no daily medications.

# Current Findings
Generalized mild-to-moderate marginal gingival inflammation with localized bleeding on probing,
moderate plaque accumulation concentrated along the gingival margin and interproximal surfaces
consistent with shortened/incomplete home brushing, and no active caries noted on visual exam.
Patient becomes visibly tense (rigid posture, hands over ears) when unfamiliar instruments are
introduced without warning.
