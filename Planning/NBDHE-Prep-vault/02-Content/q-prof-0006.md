# Stem
All of the following are typical dental practice act supervision categories that define the level
of dentist oversight required for a dental hygienist to perform a given procedure EXCEPT:

# Options
- A) reciprocal supervision (correct)
- B) direct supervision
- C) general supervision
- D) indirect supervision

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Most state dental practice acts define hygienist scope of practice using categories such as
direct supervision (dentist present in the office and personally diagnoses/authorizes/checks the
procedure), indirect supervision (dentist present in the office but not necessarily observing),
and general supervision (dentist has authorized the treatment but need not be on-site). "Reciprocal
supervision" is not a recognized supervision-level term in dental practice acts.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Direct supervision is a standard, widely used supervision category.
- C) General supervision is a standard, widely used supervision category.
- D) Indirect supervision is a standard, widely used supervision category.
