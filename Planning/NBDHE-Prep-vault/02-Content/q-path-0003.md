# Stem
Leukoplakia is clinically defined as a white plaque that:

# Options
- A) cannot be scraped off and cannot be attributed to any other identifiable disease (correct)
- B) wipes away easily with gauze, revealing an erythematous base
- C) is always histologically diagnostic of squamous cell carcinoma
- D) resolves spontaneously within 24–48 hours without treatment

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Leukoplakia is a clinical diagnosis of exclusion: a white patch or plaque that cannot be rubbed
off and cannot be characterized clinically or pathologically as any other definable lesion. Some
lesions show epithelial dysplasia and carry premalignant potential, so biopsy is indicated.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) A white lesion that wipes off to reveal a red base describes pseudomembranous candidiasis
  (thrush), not leukoplakia.
- C) Leukoplakia is a clinical term, not a histologic one; only biopsy can determine whether
  dysplasia or carcinoma is actually present.
- D) Leukoplakia is a persistent lesion by definition; a white lesion that resolves quickly after
  removing an irritant (e.g., traumatic keratosis) is not classified as leukoplakia.
