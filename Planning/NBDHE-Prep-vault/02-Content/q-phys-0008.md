# Stem
When serum calcium falls, the parathyroid glands release parathyroid hormone (PTH), which raises
blood calcium primarily by ______.

# Options
- A) stimulating osteoclastic resorption of bone (correct)
- B) increasing calcitonin secretion from the thyroid
- C) promoting calcium excretion by the kidneys
- D) inhibiting activation of vitamin D in the kidney

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
PTH acts on osteoblasts/stromal cells to upregulate RANKL, which activates osteoclasts to resorb
bone and release stored calcium into the circulation. PTH also increases renal tubular
reabsorption of calcium and stimulates renal 1α-hydroxylase to produce active vitamin D
(1,25-(OH)₂D), which in turn increases intestinal calcium absorption — all three effects raise
blood calcium.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Calcitonin *lowers* blood calcium (it inhibits osteoclasts) and is not stimulated by PTH — the
  opposite hormonal relationship.
- C) PTH *increases* renal calcium reabsorption, reducing urinary calcium loss, not promoting
  excretion.
- D) PTH *activates* (not inhibits) the renal enzyme that produces active vitamin D, which further
  raises calcium by boosting intestinal absorption.
