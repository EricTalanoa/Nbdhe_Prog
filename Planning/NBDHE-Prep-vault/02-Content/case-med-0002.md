# Demographics
Female, age 68. History of osteoporosis treated with weekly oral alendronate for the past 6 years;
also completed intravenous zoledronic acid infusions during earlier treatment for breast cancer.
Otherwise well-controlled health. No known drug allergies.

# Chief Complaint
"The spot where my back tooth was pulled two months ago never healed and now I can feel rough bone."

# Background / History
Tooth #30 was extracted about 8 weeks ago by another provider. The patient reports mild discomfort
and a bad taste but no fever. She was not told about any special risk before the extraction. Brushes
twice daily; irregular flossing. Nonsmoker.

# Current Findings
An area of **exposed, rough, non-healing bone** is present at the #30 extraction site, without
complete soft-tissue coverage after 8 weeks; surrounding mucosa is mildly erythematous, minimal
purulence, no facial swelling. Generalized mild gingival inflammation elsewhere; moderate
supragingival calculus on the mandibular anteriors. Remaining dentition is otherwise intact with no
new caries.

# Notes
Original case authored to the blueprint (Rule 0) — no real patient or exam data. Two linked items
(q-med-0004 recognizing/managing MRONJ and its implication for care planning, q-phar-0004 the drug
mechanism behind the impaired healing) draw on this patient box. The history deliberately includes
**both** oral and IV antiresorptive exposure so the higher-risk IV route can be distinguished from
the lower-risk oral route.
