# Stem
Before designing an intervention for a community oral-health program, the dental hygienist's first
step should be to:

# Options
- A) conduct a needs assessment to identify the population's existing oral health status and resources (correct)
- B) select and purchase preventive materials such as fluoride varnish for distribution
- C) write the program's final evaluation report
- D) recruit community volunteers to deliver the planned intervention

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Community program planning follows a needs-assessment-first sequence: a needs (or community)
assessment gathers data on the population's oral health status, risk factors, and available resources
so the program's goals, target group, and interventions can be matched to actual, documented needs
rather than assumptions. Only after this diagnostic step should specific interventions and materials
be selected.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Selecting materials without first assessing needs risks mismatching resources to the population's
  actual problems and priorities.
- C) Evaluation is the final stage of the planning cycle (assess → plan → implement → evaluate), not
  the first.
- D) Recruiting volunteers to deliver an intervention comes during implementation, which follows
  assessment and program design.
