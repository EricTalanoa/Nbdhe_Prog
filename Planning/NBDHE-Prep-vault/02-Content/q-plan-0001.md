# Stem
An otherwise healthy patient becomes pale, lightheaded, and briefly faints in the dental chair. The
most appropriate immediate management is to:

# Options
- A) sit the patient fully upright and offer a glass of water
- B) position the patient supine with the legs slightly elevated and ensure an open airway (correct)
- C) administer sublingual nitroglycerin
- D) begin chest compressions

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Vasovagal syncope is a transient loss of consciousness from reduced cerebral blood flow. Placing the
patient supine with legs slightly raised restores cerebral perfusion; the clinician then ensures an
open airway, monitors breathing and pulse, and may use an ammonia inhalant.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Sitting the patient upright reduces cerebral perfusion and can prolong or deepen the faint.
- C) Nitroglycerin lowers blood pressure and is indicated for angina, which would worsen syncope.
- D) Chest compressions are for a pulseless patient; a fainting patient retains a pulse.
