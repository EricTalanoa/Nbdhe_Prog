# Stem
A recurrent, shallow, painful ulcer with a yellow-white fibrinous center and an erythematous halo,
confined to nonkeratinized oral mucosa, is most characteristic of:

# Options
- A) a recurrent aphthous ulcer (canker sore) (correct)
- B) a herpetic vesicle from primary herpetic gingivostomatitis
- C) linea alba
- D) an amalgam tattoo

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Recurrent aphthous ulcers present as painful, shallow ulcerations with a yellow-white fibrinous
pseudomembrane and a red halo, typically on movable, nonkeratinized mucosa (buccal/labial mucosa,
soft palate, ventral tongue), and they recur episodically.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Herpetic lesions begin as small vesicles that rupture into clusters of tiny ulcers, classically
  on keratinized tissue (attached gingiva, hard palate) in intraoral recurrent herpes, and are
  preceded by a prodrome.
- C) Linea alba is a white, asymptomatic keratotic line on the buccal mucosa at the occlusal plane
  from chronic frictional trauma, not an ulcer.
- D) An amalgam tattoo is a flat, painless, blue-gray pigmented macule from embedded amalgam
  particles, not an ulcerated lesion.
