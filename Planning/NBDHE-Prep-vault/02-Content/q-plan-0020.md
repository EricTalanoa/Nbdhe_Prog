# Stem
A well-sequenced case presentation discusses the clinical findings and diagnosis with the patient
before presenting the specific treatment recommendations, primarily so that the patient:

# Options
- A) can compare fee schedules across the recommended options before hearing about the problem
- B) understands the problem being addressed before evaluating the proposed solution, supporting informed decision-making (correct)
- C) has time to schedule follow-up appointments before treatment is discussed
- D) can select specific product brands before treatment options are explained

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Presenting findings and diagnosis first gives the patient the clinical context needed to make sense
of why a treatment is being recommended. Reversing that order — leading with treatment before the
patient understands the problem — undermines informed consent, since a patient can't meaningfully
weigh options for a problem they haven't yet been shown.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Fee schedules and payment logistics are administrative details, not the reason findings
  precede treatment in a case presentation.
- C) Appointment scheduling is a logistical step that follows the clinical conversation, not the
  purpose of the sequencing.
- D) Product selection is a minor implementation detail, not the rationale for discussing findings
  and diagnosis before treatment recommendations.
