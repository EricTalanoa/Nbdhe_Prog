# Stem
When sequencing a dental hygiene care plan for a patient with generalized moderate periodontitis
and several active carious lesions, treatment should typically be ordered so that ___.

# Options
- A) definitive restorative and esthetic procedures are completed before any periodontal debridement begins
- B) disease-control procedures — oral hygiene instruction and nonsurgical periodontal therapy — precede elective restorative and esthetic procedures (correct)
- C) periodontal surgery is performed in all affected sextants before any nonsurgical therapy is attempted
- D) maintenance recall intervals are set before active therapy has begun

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Standard care-plan sequencing addresses the disease-control phase — patient education,
scaling/root planing, and caries control — before the corrective/restorative phase and the
maintenance phase. Controlling periodontal inflammation and active caries first improves tissue
health and gives more predictable, stable results for any restorative work that follows.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Restoring teeth before disease control risks placing margins against actively inflamed,
  bleeding tissue and leaves periodontal disease to progress untreated.
- C) Surgical therapy is reserved for sites that remain inflamed after reassessment of
  nonsurgical therapy, not performed first.
- D) Maintenance intervals are established after active therapy achieves a stable result, not
  before treatment starts.
