# Stem
Under current American Heart Association guidelines, antibiotic prophylaxis before an invasive
dental procedure is indicated for a patient with:

# Options
- A) a prosthetic (artificial) heart valve (correct)
- B) uncomplicated mitral valve prolapse without regurgitation
- C) a coronary artery stent placed two years ago
- D) well-controlled essential hypertension

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Since 2007 the AHA restricts prophylaxis to the cardiac conditions at highest risk for an adverse
outcome from infective endocarditis: prosthetic valves or prosthetic valve material, previous
infective endocarditis, certain congenital heart defects, and cardiac transplant recipients with
valvulopathy. A prosthetic heart valve qualifies.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Mitral valve prolapse is no longer an indication for prophylaxis.
- C) Coronary stents do not require antibiotic prophylaxis for dental care.
- D) Hypertension is unrelated to infective-endocarditis risk and needs no prophylaxis.
