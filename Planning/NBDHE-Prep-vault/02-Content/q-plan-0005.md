# Stem
All of the following are appropriate applications of standard infection-control practice in the
operatory EXCEPT:

# Options
- A) using a heat-sterilized, biologically monitored instrument cassette for each patient
- B) wearing gloves, mask, and protective eyewear during instrumentation with an ultrasonic scaler
- C) wiping down and reusing the same pair of gloves between patients to conserve supplies (correct)
- D) placing surface barriers on the light handle and chair switch and changing them between patients

# Correct answer
C

# Rationale
Gloves are single-use, single-patient items under standard precautions; they must be removed and
replaced with a fresh pair for each patient (and hand hygiene performed), never wiped down and
reused, because they cannot be reliably disinfected while intact and their integrity degrades with
reuse.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Heat sterilization with routine biological (spore) monitoring is the standard for critical/
  semicritical instruments and is appropriate infection control.
- B) Aerosol-generating procedures like ultrasonic scaling require full PPE (gloves, mask,
  eyewear) under standard precautions.
- D) Surface barriers on frequently touched items, changed between patients, are an appropriate and
  recommended infection-control application.
