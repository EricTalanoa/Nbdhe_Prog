# Demographics
Male, age 14. Healthy, no medications, no known allergies. Lives in an optimally fluoridated
community. Fixed labial orthodontic appliances (brackets and archwires) on both arches, placed
14 months ago; estimated 10 months of treatment remaining.

# Chief Complaint
"My orthodontist saw some white marks around my brackets and told me to get them checked."

# Background / History
Referred by his orthodontist for a hygiene visit and caries-risk evaluation. Brushes once a day,
quickly, and finds it hard to clean around the brackets; does not use floss threaders or an
interdental brush. High-frequency intake of sports drinks and crackers throughout the day,
especially during after-school sports. No prior restorative treatment.

# Current Findings
Generalized plaque biofilm accumulation around bracket bases and along the gingival margin.
Marginal gingivitis with mild gingival enlargement adjacent to the appliances; bleeding on probing
at scattered facial sites, probing depths ≤ 3 mm with no attachment loss. **Chalky, opaque
white-spot lesions on the facial cervical thirds of #7, #8, #9, and #10**, adjacent to the bracket
margins; surfaces are intact and non-cavitated. No frank cavitation elsewhere.
