# Stem
As this 24-week-pregnant patient is reclined for scaling, she becomes lightheaded, pale, and
nauseated. What is the most appropriate immediate positioning adjustment?

# Options
- A) roll her onto her left side or place a wedge under her right hip to displace the uterus (correct)
- B) lay her fully supine and flat to increase blood flow to the brain
- C) sit her fully upright and continue treatment
- D) place her in a steep head-down (Trendelenburg) position

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
These are signs of **supine hypotensive syndrome**: in the second and third trimesters, lying the
patient flat lets the gravid uterus compress the inferior vena cava, reducing venous return,
cardiac output, and cerebral perfusion. Rolling her onto her **left side** — or tilting her by
placing a wedge/rolled towel under the **right** hip — shifts the uterus off the vena cava and
restores venous return, usually relieving symptoms promptly.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) A fully supine, flat position is exactly what **causes** the vena cava compression; it would
  worsen, not relieve, the hypotension.
- C) Bolting her fully upright does not address vena cava compression and can provoke orthostatic
  lightheadedness; the targeted fix is lateral displacement of the uterus, and treatment should
  pause until she recovers.
- D) A steep head-down position increases pressure on the diaphragm and does not relieve caval
  compression by the uterus; it is not indicated here.
