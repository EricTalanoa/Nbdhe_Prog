# Stem
In periodontitis, the actual destruction of gingival collagen and alveolar bone is caused most
directly by which of the following?

# Options
- A) host-derived matrix metalloproteinases and inflammatory mediators (correct)
- B) bacterial lipopolysaccharide dissolving collagen directly
- C) salivary immunoglobulin A
- D) mechanical pressure from subgingival calculus

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
The subgingival biofilm **initiates** disease, but most tissue breakdown is **host-mediated**. In
response to bacterial products, immune and resident cells release matrix metalloproteinases (MMPs,
especially collagenases) that degrade the collagen matrix, and mediators such as IL-1, TNF-α, and
prostaglandin E2 that drive osteoclastic bone resorption. This is why periodontitis is understood
as a dysregulated host inflammatory response, not simple bacterial digestion of tissue.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Lipopolysaccharide (endotoxin) is a potent **trigger** of the host inflammatory response, but
  it does not itself enzymatically dissolve collagen; the collagenolysis comes from host MMPs.
- C) Salivary IgA is a **protective** immune factor that blocks microbial adherence; it does not
  destroy periodontal tissue.
- D) Calculus is a plaque-retentive surface that perpetuates inflammation, but the destruction is
  driven by the inflammatory response it harbors, not by mechanical pressure from the deposit.
