# Stem
Which group of bacteria is most strongly associated with progressive chronic periodontitis and
increasing probing depth?

# Options
- A) Streptococcus mutans and Lactobacillus species
- B) the "red complex": Porphyromonas gingivalis, Tannerella forsythia, and Treponema denticola (correct)
- C) Actinomyces viscosus and Actinomyces naeslundii
- D) Streptococcus sanguinis and Streptococcus mitis

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
The red complex organisms — P. gingivalis, T. forsythia, and T. denticola — are late colonizers of
subgingival biofilm that are strongly and consistently associated with deepening pockets, bleeding
on probing, and progressive attachment loss in chronic periodontitis.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) S. mutans and Lactobacillus are the primary cariogenic organisms associated with dental
  caries, not periodontal destruction.
- C) Actinomyces species are early/supragingival plaque colonizers associated more with gingivitis
  and calculus formation than with periodontal disease progression.
- D) S. sanguinis and S. mitis are early, health-associated pioneer colonizers of the pellicle, not
  markers of periodontal pathogenesis.
