# Stem
For a patient with a prosthetic heart valve and no penicillin allergy, the standard antibiotic
prophylaxis regimen before scaling and root planing is:

# Options
- A) amoxicillin 2 g orally, 30–60 minutes before the procedure (correct)
- B) amoxicillin 500 mg orally, immediately after the procedure
- C) clindamycin 600 mg orally, 30–60 minutes before the procedure
- D) penicillin V 2 g intramuscularly, on the day of the procedure

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Current guidelines recommend a single dose of amoxicillin 2 g by mouth, taken 30–60 minutes before
a procedure likely to cause bacteremia, for patients with the highest-risk cardiac conditions
(including prosthetic heart valves).

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Prophylaxis must be on board before the bacteremia-producing procedure begins; dosing after
  the procedure does not protect against the bacteremia that already occurred.
- C) Clindamycin is the alternative regimen reserved for patients with a true penicillin allergy,
  not the first-line drug for a non-allergic patient.
- D) The standard regimen is oral amoxicillin, not intramuscular penicillin V; IM/IV regimens are
  reserved for patients who cannot take oral medication.
