# Stem
In a community oral-health assessment, the DMFT index quantifies a population's:

# Options
- A) dental caries experience — decayed, missing, and filled teeth (correct)
- B) level of gingival inflammation
- C) amount of plaque accumulation
- D) severity of dental fluorosis

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
The DMFT index sums Decayed, Missing (due to caries), and Filled Teeth to express a group's
cumulative caries experience (DMFS does the same by tooth surface). It is a core epidemiologic
measure for planning and evaluating community caries programs.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Gingival inflammation is measured by indices such as the Gingival Index.
- C) Plaque is measured by plaque indices (e.g., the Plaque Index), not DMFT.
- D) Fluorosis severity is scored by indices such as Dean's Fluorosis Index.
