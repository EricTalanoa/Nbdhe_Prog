# Stem
All of the following factors primarily affect radiographic contrast EXCEPT:

# Options
- A) kilovoltage (kVp)
- B) beam filtration
- C) exposure time (correct)
- D) subject density/thickness

# Correct answer
C

# Rationale
Contrast is the difference in density between adjacent areas of a radiograph and is governed
mainly by beam quality (kVp) and filtration, which set how much the beam's energy spectrum
penetrates versus scatters, along with the density/thickness of the tissue being imaged.
Exposure time instead controls the quantity of photons reaching the receptor, which primarily
changes overall image density (how dark/light the whole image is), not the contrast scale.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Higher kVp increases beam penetration and produces a longer gray scale (lower contrast);
  it is a primary contrast factor.
- B) Filtration removes lower-energy photons, hardening the beam and altering contrast.
- D) Denser or thicker tissue absorbs more of the beam, changing the contrast between structures.
