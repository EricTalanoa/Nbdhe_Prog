# Demographics
Male, age 58. 40 pack-year cigarette history; drinks 3–4 alcoholic beverages daily. No routine
dental care for several years. No known drug allergies; no relevant systemic disease reported.

# Chief Complaint
"There's a sore spot on the side of my tongue that's been there over a month and won't go away."

# Background / History
Reports the area is not particularly painful but "feels rough." No recent trauma, sharp teeth, or
new restorations at the site. No fever or systemic symptoms. Presents primarily for the tongue
lesion after a family member urged him to get it checked.

# Current Findings
On the **lateral border of the tongue** there is a ~1.5 cm ulcerated lesion with mixed red and white
(erythroleukoplakic) surface changes and **firm, indurated (rolled) margins**; it does not wipe off
and has been present for more than three weeks. No obvious source of mechanical trauma at the site.
A firm, non-tender ipsilateral submandibular lymph node is palpable. Generalized moderate calculus
and mild gingival inflammation; multiple restorations, no acute caries emergencies.

# Notes
Original case authored to the blueprint (Rule 0) — no real patient or exam data. Two linked items
(q-asmt-0013 the most appropriate action for a persistent indurated lesion, q-path-0007 high-risk
site and premalignant pattern recognition) draw on this patient box. The lesion is described with
the classic worrisome features — persistence >2 weeks, induration, erythroleukoplakia, high-risk
site, tobacco/alcohol history, and a firm node — so the intended answer is referral/biopsy, not
watchful waiting.
