# Stem
The patient's blood pressure at today's appointment is 186/114 mm Hg and he reports running out of
his antihypertensive medication. What is the most appropriate course of action for this visit?

# Options
- A) defer elective treatment, advise him to contact his physician for medical management, and arrange urgent evaluation (correct)
- B) proceed with scaling and root planing as planned, using extra epinephrine for hemostasis
- C) proceed with treatment but omit local anesthetic entirely
- D) ignore the reading because he has no symptoms, and rebook in six months

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
A blood pressure at or above roughly **180/110 mm Hg** is a level at which **elective dental and
dental-hygiene treatment should be deferred**; the risk of an acute cardiovascular event outweighs
any benefit of elective care that day. The appropriate action is to postpone elective treatment,
inform the patient of the reading, and refer him for prompt medical evaluation and re-establishment
of his antihypertensive therapy. Only urgent/emergency dental needs would be managed, with medical
consultation.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Proceeding with elective care is contraindicated at this pressure, and **adding epinephrine**
  can further raise blood pressure — exactly the wrong move in a severely hypertensive patient.
- C) The problem is not the anesthetic choice; elective treatment itself should be deferred at this
  pressure, so simply omitting local anesthetic does not make the appointment safe.
- D) Being asymptomatic does not make a reading this high safe, and a 6-month rebook ignores both a
  medical red flag and his active periodontal disease.
