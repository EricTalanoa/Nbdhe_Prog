# Stem
Stimulation of which branch of the autonomic nervous system produces a profuse, watery salivary
secretion?

# Options
- A) parasympathetic (correct)
- B) sympathetic
- C) somatic motor
- D) enteric

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Parasympathetic stimulation, carried by cranial nerves VII and IX, dilates salivary gland blood
vessels and drives a copious, serous, watery secretion important for swallowing, digestion, and
maintaining a healthy oral environment.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Sympathetic stimulation constricts glandular vessels and yields a scant, thick, mucous-rich
  secretion, not a copious watery flow.
- C) The somatic motor system innervates skeletal muscle and has no direct role in glandular
  secretion.
- D) The enteric nervous system governs gastrointestinal motility and secretion, not salivary
  gland output.
