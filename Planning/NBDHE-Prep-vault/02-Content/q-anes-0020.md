# Stem
A patient is scheduled for extensive quadrant scaling and root planing expected to take about
90 minutes, followed by an anticipated need for postoperative pain control into the evening. Which
local anesthetic is the best choice for the mandibular block to cover both the procedure and the
early postoperative period?

# Options
- A) bupivacaine with epinephrine 1:200,000 (correct)
- B) mepivacaine 3% plain
- C) lidocaine 2% with epinephrine 1:100,000
- D) prilocaine 4% plain

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Bupivacaine is a long-acting amide anesthetic, with pulpal anesthesia lasting roughly 90–180
minutes and soft-tissue anesthesia extending several hours beyond that, making it well suited to
long procedures where extended postoperative analgesia is also desired.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Mepivacaine 3% plain has no vasoconstrictor, giving only a short pulpal duration
  (roughly 20–40 minutes) that would not reliably cover a 90-minute procedure, let alone provide
  postoperative coverage.
- C) Lidocaine with epinephrine gives intermediate duration (about 60 minutes of pulpal
  anesthesia), which may not comfortably cover the full procedure and offers little postoperative
  benefit compared with a long-acting agent.
- D) Prilocaine 4% plain is also short-to-intermediate acting and, without a vasoconstrictor, is
  the least likely of the choices to last through a long procedure or into the evening.
