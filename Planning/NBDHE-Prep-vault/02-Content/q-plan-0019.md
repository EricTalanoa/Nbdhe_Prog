# Stem
A properly written dental hygiene diagnosis statement links the patient's problem to its:

# Options
- A) etiology or contributing risk factor, supported by the clinical signs and symptoms observed (correct)
- B) insurance coverage category and expected reimbursement code
- C) radiographic image alone, without reference to clinical findings
- D) restorative treatment sequence planned by the dentist

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
A dental hygiene diagnosis is commonly structured as Problem-Etiology-Signs/symptoms (PES): the
identified problem is linked to its likely cause or risk factor and supported by the objective
clinical findings. This structure distinguishes a dental hygiene diagnosis from a general dental or
periodontal diagnosis and directly guides an individualized plan.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Reimbursement coding is an administrative matter, not part of a clinical diagnostic statement.
- C) A diagnosis draws on the full clinical picture; radiographs alone are insufficient.
- D) A dental hygiene diagnosis addresses hygiene-related problems, not the dentist's restorative
  sequence.
