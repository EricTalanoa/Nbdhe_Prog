# Stem
Which of the following is written as a properly structured dental hygiene diagnosis (unmet need,
related etiology, and supporting evidence)?

# Options
- A) "Patient has moderate chronic periodontitis, stage II grade B."
- B) "Deficit in the human need for skin/mucous membrane integrity related to inadequate plaque removal, as evidenced by generalized bleeding on probing and 4-5 mm pockets." (correct)
- C) "Scale and root plane teeth #2-15 using local anesthesia."
- D) "Recommend the patient purchase a power toothbrush."

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
A dental hygiene diagnosis names an unmet human need, links it to its likely etiology or
contributing factor ("related to"), and cites the clinical findings that support it ("as
evidenced by"). Option B does all three — it identifies the deficit, its cause, and the objective
findings — which is what distinguishes a diagnosis from a clinical finding or a treatment
instruction.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) States a dental/periodontal diagnosis (disease stage and grade), which is within the
  dentist's/periodontist's scope, not a human-needs-based dental hygiene diagnosis.
- C) Describes a treatment intervention (a procedure to perform), not a diagnostic statement of
  patient need.
- D) Is a product recommendation, not a diagnosis — it doesn't identify an unmet need, etiology,
  or supporting evidence.
