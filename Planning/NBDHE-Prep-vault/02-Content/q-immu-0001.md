# Stem
The predominant immunoglobulin in saliva that helps prevent microbial adherence to oral surfaces is:

# Options
- A) IgG
- B) IgM
- C) secretory IgA (correct)
- D) IgE

# Correct answer
C

# Rationale
Secretory IgA is the main antibody in saliva and other mucosal secretions. It agglutinates
microorganisms and blocks their attachment to mucosa and tooth surfaces, an important first line of
mucosal defense.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) IgG predominates in serum and in gingival crevicular fluid, not in saliva.
- B) IgM is the first antibody produced in a primary response and is largely confined to the
  bloodstream.
- D) IgE mediates allergic and antiparasitic responses.
