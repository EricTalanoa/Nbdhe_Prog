# Stem
A dental hygiene diagnosis, as distinct from a dental/periodontal diagnosis, is best described as a
statement that identifies:

# Options
- A) the specific periodontal disease stage and grade present
- B) an unmet human need related to oral health that dental hygiene interventions can address (correct)
- C) the restorative or surgical procedures required to treat disease
- D) the ICD-10 code that will be submitted for reimbursement

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
The dental hygiene diagnosis identifies an existing or potential oral health problem framed as an
unmet human need (e.g., a need for skin/mucous membrane integrity, or for conceptualization and
understanding) that falls within the hygienist's scope and can be resolved or improved through
dental hygiene care planning and interventions.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Staging and grading periodontitis is part of the dentist's/periodontist's clinical diagnosis,
  not the framework for a dental hygiene diagnosis.
- C) Prescribing restorative or surgical treatment is outside the dental hygiene scope of practice
  and diagnosis.
- D) A billing code is an administrative artifact, not a diagnostic statement of patient need.
