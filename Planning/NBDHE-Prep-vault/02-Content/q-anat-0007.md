# Stem
Which term names the surface of a posterior tooth that faces the tongue?

# Options
- A) buccal
- B) facial
- C) lingual (correct)
- D) incisal

# Correct answer
C

# Rationale
"Lingual" designates the tooth surface oriented toward the tongue, used for both maxillary and
mandibular posterior (and anterior) teeth.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Buccal names the surface facing the cheek on posterior teeth, the opposite side from lingual.
- B) Facial is the general term for the surface facing the lips/cheeks (buccal or labial), not the
  tongue side.
- D) Incisal names the biting edge of anterior teeth, not a facial/lingual surface designation, and
  posterior teeth don't have an incisal surface at all (they have an occlusal surface).
