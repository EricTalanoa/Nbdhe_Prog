# Stem
A patient with severe, long-standing dental phobia agrees to a graduated care plan: the first visit
is only a seat-and-tour of the operatory, the second adds a mirror exam with no instruments, and
later visits progressively introduce sound, then touch, then instrumentation, only once the patient
reports feeling calm at each step. This behavior-management approach is best identified as:

# Options
- A) systematic desensitization (correct)
- B) tell-show-do
- C) voice control
- D) positive reinforcement

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Systematic desensitization pairs relaxation with a graduated hierarchy of anxiety-provoking stimuli,
advancing to the next step only after the patient tolerates the current one calmly, across multiple
visits. That staged, multi-appointment structure — not a single pre-procedure demonstration — is
the defining feature described here.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Tell-show-do is a single-visit technique (explain, demonstrate on a model, then perform); it
  does not describe a multi-visit hierarchy of exposure steps.
- C) Voice control uses tone, pace, and firmness during a procedure to gain attention and
  cooperation; it is not a graduated exposure plan.
- D) Positive reinforcement rewards desired behavior after it occurs and can be used within any
  technique, but it does not itself describe the staged exposure hierarchy in the vignette.
