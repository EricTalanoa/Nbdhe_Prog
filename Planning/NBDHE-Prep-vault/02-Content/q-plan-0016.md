# Stem
The primary clinical reason periodontal maintenance appointments are scheduled at 3- to 4-month
intervals rather than a standard 6-month recall is that:

# Options
- A) periodontal pathogens can repopulate subgingival pockets and reach disease-associated levels within about 3 months after therapy (correct)
- B) dental insurance plans require quarterly visits for coverage to continue
- C) fluoride varnish must be reapplied every 3 months to prevent root caries
- D) full-mouth radiographs are needed more frequently to monitor alveolar bone levels

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Studies of subgingival microbial recolonization show that periodontal pathogens can return to
pre-treatment, disease-associated levels within roughly 9-11 weeks in periodontally susceptible
patients. A 3- to 4-month interval interrupts this recolonization before it can re-trigger active
disease — the clinical rationale for shortening the maintenance interval below a routine prophylaxis
schedule.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Insurance coverage policy is not the clinical or scientific basis for the interval and should
  not be the reason given in patient education.
- C) Fluoride reapplication schedules are not what determines the periodontal maintenance interval.
- D) Radiographic monitoring follows a separate, much longer schedule and is not why the clinical
  recall interval is shortened.
