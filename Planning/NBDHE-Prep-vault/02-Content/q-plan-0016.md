# Stem
When educating a patient newly diagnosed with chronic periodontitis, in contrast to gingivitis
alone, the hygienist should emphasize that periodontitis involves:

# Options
- A) inflammation confined to the gingiva that resolves completely with improved brushing
- B) loss of periodontal attachment and supporting bone that will not spontaneously reverse with home care alone (correct)
- C) a bacterial infection limited to the enamel surface
- D) a temporary increase in probing depths that returns to baseline once plaque is removed

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Gingivitis is reversible inflammation limited to the gingiva. Periodontitis, by definition, includes
loss of clinical attachment and alveolar bone — damage that improved oral hygiene alone cannot
regenerate, which is why the patient needs to understand active professional therapy and ongoing
maintenance are required to control, not just reverse, the disease.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Describes gingivitis, the reversible precursor condition, not periodontitis.
- C) Periodontal disease is a biofilm-driven infection of the supporting structures, not an enamel
  surface process.
- D) Attachment and bone loss in periodontitis persist after plaque control; probing depths may
  improve with therapy, but lost attachment does not return to pre-disease baseline on its own.
