# Stem
Before placing a resin-based pit-and-fissure sealant, all of the following steps are appropriate
EXCEPT:

# Options
- A) cleaning the occlusal surface and isolating the tooth to maintain a dry field
- B) etching the enamel with phosphoric acid, then rinsing and drying to a frosty appearance
- C) applying a layer of fluoride varnish directly beneath the sealant to boost retention (correct)
- D) confirming the etched surface stays free of saliva contamination before applying the sealant

# Correct answer
C

# Rationale
This is a negative-format item — the key is the step that is NOT part of correct sealant technique.
Fluoride varnish is not applied beneath a sealant; a film of varnish (or any contaminant) between
the etched enamel and the resin would block the resin tags from penetrating the etched surface and
compromise retention.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Cleaning and maintaining a dry, isolated field is a required first step for good bonding.
- B) Acid-etching to a frosty, matte appearance creates the microporosities the resin tags flow
  into, essential for mechanical retention.
- D) Saliva contamination of the etched surface is a leading cause of sealant failure, so confirming
  a dry field before applying the sealant is standard technique.
