# Stem
Once his blood pressure is controlled and he is medically cleared, which phase of care is the most
appropriate **first** therapeutic step for this patient?

# Options
- A) nonsurgical periodontal therapy with caries stabilization and oral-hygiene instruction (the disease-control phase) (correct)
- B) periodontal surgery to correct the vertical bony defects
- C) definitive restorations and fixed prosthetics for the questionable teeth
- D) placing him on a routine 6-month recall and reassessing then

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Care is sequenced **emergency → disease control → definitive → maintenance**. With active
periodontitis and caries but no acute emergency, the first therapeutic phase is **disease control
(initial/nonsurgical therapy)**: scaling and root planing, removal of local factors, caries
stabilization, and oral-hygiene instruction, followed by re-evaluation. This controls the active
infection and lets the tissues respond before any decision about surgery or definitive restorative
work.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Periodontal surgery is a **later** decision, made at re-evaluation only if pockets and defects
  persist after nonsurgical therapy — it is not the first step.
- C) Definitive restorations and prosthetics belong to the definitive phase, after the periodontal
  infection and caries activity are controlled; restoring on an inflamed, active foundation
  compromises the result.
- D) A 6-month recall is a maintenance interval for a stable, low-risk patient; deferring active
  disease for six months allows continued destruction and is inappropriate here.
