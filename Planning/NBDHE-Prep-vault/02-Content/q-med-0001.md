# Stem
Before beginning scaling and root planing for this patient taking warfarin, the most appropriate
step is to:

# Options
- A) obtain a recent INR and consult her physician as needed, without independently stopping the warfarin (correct)
- B) instruct her to stop the warfarin for three days beforehand
- C) proceed right away, since scaling is non-invasive and cannot cause meaningful bleeding
- D) have her double the warfarin dose to prevent clots during treatment

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
For an anticoagulated patient, confirm a current INR (ideally within about 24-72 hours) and
coordinate with the physician. When the INR is within the therapeutic range, scaling and root
planing can usually proceed with local hemostatic measures. The dental hygienist never alters
anticoagulation independently — stopping warfarin risks a thromboembolic event such as a stroke.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Discontinuing warfarin is a physician's decision and can precipitate a dangerous clot; it is
  not done routinely for periodontal therapy.
- C) Scaling and root planing can produce appreciable bleeding, so "non-invasive" is inaccurate
  here.
- D) Increasing the dose raises bleeding risk and is outside the hygienist's scope.
