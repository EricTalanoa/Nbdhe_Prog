# Stem
On a dental radiograph, which normal tooth structure appears the most radiopaque?

# Options
- A) enamel (correct)
- B) dental pulp
- C) the periodontal ligament space
- D) dentin

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Radiopacity reflects how much a structure attenuates the X-ray beam, which tracks with density and
mineral content. Enamel is the most highly mineralized tissue in the body, so it absorbs the most
radiation and appears the whitest (most radiopaque) on a radiograph.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) The pulp is soft tissue and appears radiolucent (dark).
- C) The periodontal ligament space is a thin radiolucent line around the root.
- D) Dentin is mineralized but less so than enamel, so it appears less radiopaque.
