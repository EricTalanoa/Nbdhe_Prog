# Stem
Fully informing a patient of the risks, benefits, and alternatives of a proposed treatment so they
can decide for themselves chiefly upholds the ethical principle of:

# Options
- A) beneficence
- B) nonmaleficence
- C) autonomy (correct)
- D) justice

# Correct answer
C

# Rationale
Respecting a patient's right to make an informed decision about their own care is the principle of
autonomy, operationalized through the informed-consent process.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Beneficence is acting in the patient's best interest (doing good).
- B) Nonmaleficence is the duty to "do no harm."
- D) Justice concerns fairness in the distribution of care and resources.
