# Stem
A community program designer wants to increase sealant uptake by addressing parents' belief that
their child is unlikely to get cavities. Which health behavior model construct is this targeting?

# Options
- A) perceived susceptibility (correct)
- B) perceived barriers
- C) cues to action
- D) self-efficacy

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
The Health Belief Model's perceived susceptibility construct describes a person's subjective sense
of their (or their child's) risk of developing a condition. Parents who underestimate their child's
caries risk have low perceived susceptibility, and targeting that belief directly (e.g., with risk
information) is the relevant lever for increasing preventive-service uptake such as sealants.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Perceived barriers refers to the practical obstacles to acting (cost, access, time), not to
  disbelief about personal risk.
- C) Cues to action are external or internal prompts that trigger the decision to act (e.g., a
  reminder postcard), which is a different mechanism than correcting a risk misperception.
- D) Self-efficacy is confidence in one's own ability to carry out the behavior successfully, not
  the belief about how likely the disease is to occur.
