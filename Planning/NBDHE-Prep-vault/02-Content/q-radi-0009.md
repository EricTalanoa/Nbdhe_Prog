# Stem
Compared with conventional film, a direct digital radiography sensor primarily allows the operator
to:

# Options
- A) eliminate the need for a lead apron and thyroid collar entirely
- B) view the image immediately and reduce patient dose through lower exposure requirements (correct)
- C) obtain a three-dimensional volumetric reconstruction from a single exposure
- D) avoid all cross-contamination concerns since sensors are disposable

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Digital sensors are more sensitive to X-radiation than film, so they require a shorter exposure
time for an adequate image, lowering patient dose, and the image displays on a monitor within
seconds instead of requiring chemical processing.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Digital imaging reduces dose but does not eliminate the need for standard radiation-protection
  measures like the lead apron and thyroid collar.
- C) A single conventional digital exposure still produces a two-dimensional image; volumetric
  reconstruction requires CBCT, a distinct technology.
- D) Rigid digital sensors are reusable, not disposable, and still require barrier protection and
  disinfection between patients.
