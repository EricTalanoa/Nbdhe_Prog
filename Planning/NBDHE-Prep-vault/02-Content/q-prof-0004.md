# Stem
Motivational interviewing to support a patient's behavior change relies primarily on:

# Options
- A) eliciting the patient's own reasons for change through open-ended questions and reflective listening (correct)
- B) warning the patient about consequences until they agree to comply
- C) giving every patient the same standard set of instructions
- D) setting the behavior goals for the patient in advance

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Motivational interviewing is a collaborative, patient-centered style that evokes the person's own
motivation using open-ended questions, affirmations, reflective listening, and summaries (OARS). It
respects autonomy rather than relying on confrontation or generic advice.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Warning or scare tactics are confrontational and tend to increase resistance, contrary to the
  spirit of motivational interviewing.
- C) Identical instructions for everyone are not individualized and do not evoke the patient's own
  motivation.
- D) The clinician guides but does not impose goals; the patient's autonomy in choosing is central.
