# Stem
A patient reports persistent pain from a mandibular molar during instrumentation despite a
clinically adequate inferior alveolar nerve block (lip and tongue are numb). This residual
sensation is most likely explained by accessory innervation from the:

# Options
- A) mylohyoid nerve (correct)
- B) buccal nerve
- C) greater palatine nerve
- D) infraorbital nerve

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
The mylohyoid nerve, a branch of the inferior alveolar nerve that splits off before the nerve
enters the mandibular foramen, can carry accessory sensory fibers to the mandibular molars along
the lingual aspect of the mandible. Because it branches proximal to the injection site, a standard
IANB does not anesthetize it, which can leave pulpal or lingual sensation intact even when lip and
tongue numbness confirm the main nerve trunk is blocked.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) The buccal (long buccal) nerve supplies buccal soft tissue in the molar region, not pulpal
  sensation to the teeth themselves.
- C) The greater palatine nerve innervates the posterior hard palate, unrelated to mandibular molar
  sensation.
- D) The infraorbital nerve innervates maxillary anterior/premolar structures and the midface, not
  the mandible.
