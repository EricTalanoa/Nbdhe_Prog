# Stem
When releasing a patient's protected health information to a dental insurer for claims processing,
HIPAA requires the practice to disclose:

# Options
- A) only the minimum information necessary for the purpose (correct)
- B) the patient's complete chart, to be thorough
- C) whatever is requested, as long as it is given verbally
- D) nothing at all without a separate signed authorization

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
HIPAA's "minimum necessary" standard limits disclosure of protected health information to only what
is needed for the stated purpose. Disclosures for treatment, payment, and health-care operations
are permitted, but for payment the practice still sends only the minimum necessary.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Sending the entire chart exceeds what is needed and violates the minimum-necessary standard.
- C) The manner (verbal vs written) does not change the minimum-necessary obligation, and
  disclosures should be documented.
- D) A separate signed authorization is not required for routine payment disclosures.
