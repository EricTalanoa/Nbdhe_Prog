# Stem
Alginate (irreversible hydrocolloid) impression material sets by:

# Options
- A) cooling and physical gelation
- B) an irreversible chemical cross-linking reaction (correct)
- C) evaporation of water from the mix
- D) light-curing under a curing lamp

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Alginate is an irreversible hydrocolloid: it sets through a chemical reaction (calcium ions
replacing sodium and cross-linking the alginate), which cannot be reversed by reheating. The set
impression should be poured promptly to avoid dimensional change.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Cooling and physical gelation describes reversible (agar) hydrocolloid, which can be re-melted.
- C) Alginate does not set by evaporation; premature water loss (syneresis) actually distorts it.
- D) Alginate is not a light-cured material.
