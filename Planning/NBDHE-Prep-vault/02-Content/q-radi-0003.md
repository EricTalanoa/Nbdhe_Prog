# Stem
According to the law of Bergonié and Tribondeau, the cells most sensitive to ionizing radiation are
those that are:

# Options
- A) rapidly dividing and undifferentiated (correct)
- B) mature and highly specialized
- C) in a permanent, non-dividing state
- D) large and rich in cytoplasm

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Bergonié and Tribondeau observed that cells are most radiosensitive when they are rapidly dividing,
undifferentiated, and have a long mitotic future. This is why tissues such as bone marrow,
reproductive cells, and developing tissues are especially vulnerable to radiation.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Mature, highly differentiated cells are comparatively radioresistant.
- C) Permanent non-dividing cells (e.g., mature nerve and muscle) are among the most resistant.
- D) Cell size and cytoplasm volume are not the determinants of radiosensitivity.
