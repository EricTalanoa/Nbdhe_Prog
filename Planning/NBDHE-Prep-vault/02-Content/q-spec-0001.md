# Stem
Which non-pharmacologic approach is most appropriate for reducing this nonverbal patient's anxiety
before and during instrumentation?

# Options
- A) tell-show-do paired with a consistent, predictable visit routine and advance warning before each new sensation (correct)
- B) proceed directly with instrumentation without preview, since explanations will not be understood
- C) physically hold the patient's arms still without consent to shorten the appointment
- D) reschedule indefinitely until the patient can verbally agree to treatment

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Tell-show-do — demonstrating an instrument, letting the patient see and, when tolerated, touch or
hear it before it is used — combined with a predictable sequence of steps reduces uncertainty,
which is a major anxiety trigger for many patients with autism spectrum disorder. Consistency
across visits (same order of steps, advance warning before new stimuli) builds trust over time and
increases cooperation without medication or restraint.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Skipping any preview removes predictability and is more likely to trigger a defensive or
  sensory-overload reaction, not less.
- C) Non-consensual physical restraint is inappropriate outside a documented, consented protective
  stabilization protocol and can cause physical or psychological harm; it is not a routine anxiety
  management technique.
- D) Verbal agreement is not the only valid form of assent — nonverbal patients can still receive
  care using adapted communication and behavior guidance; indefinite deferral denies needed
  preventive care.
