# Stem
Which of the following is the strongest **modifiable** risk factor for developing
peri-implantitis?

# Options
- A) Cigarette smoking (correct)
- B) A history of treated periodontitis
- C) Male sex
- D) Implant placement in the posterior mandible

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Smoking is consistently identified as the leading modifiable risk factor for peri-implant disease —
it reduces gingival vascularity and impairs the local immune response, increasing both the
likelihood and severity of peri-implant bone loss. Because a patient can change this behavior,
smoking cessation counseling is a key part of implant maintenance and risk management.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) A prior history of periodontitis is a well-established risk factor for peri-implantitis, but
  it is not modifiable — the patient cannot change their treatment history, so it doesn't fit the
  "modifiable" criterion the stem asks about.
- C) Sex is not a consistently supported risk factor for peri-implant disease and is not
  modifiable in any case.
- D) Implant location (anterior vs. posterior, maxilla vs. mandible) is not itself a major
  established risk factor for peri-implantitis, and it isn't something the patient can modify.
