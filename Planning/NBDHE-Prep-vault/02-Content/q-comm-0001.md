# Stem
The U.S. Public Health Service recommended optimal fluoride concentration for community water
fluoridation is:

# Options
- A) 0.7 ppm (correct)
- B) 1.5 ppm
- C) 4.0 ppm
- D) 0.1 ppm

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Since 2015 the U.S. Public Health Service recommends a single optimal concentration of 0.7 mg/L
(ppm) for community water fluoridation, balancing caries prevention against the risk of dental
fluorosis.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) 1.5 ppm exceeds the current U.S. recommendation and raises the risk of fluorosis.
- C) 4.0 ppm is the EPA enforceable maximum contaminant level, not an optimal preventive level.
- D) 0.1 ppm is far too low to confer meaningful caries protection.
