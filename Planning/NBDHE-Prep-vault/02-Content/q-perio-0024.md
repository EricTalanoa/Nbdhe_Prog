# Stem
A patient with generalized Stage III, Grade C periodontitis and a high bacterial load of
*Aggregatibacter actinomycetemcomitans* has completed scaling and root planing. The dentist wants
to add a short course of systemic antibiotics to improve clinical outcomes. Which combination is
most supported for this purpose?

# Options
- A) amoxicillin plus metronidazole (correct)
- B) topical chlorhexidine rinse alone, since systemic antibiotics provide no additional benefit once SRP is complete
- C) a single dose of oral penicillin V taken immediately before the SRP appointment
- D) long-term daily doxycycline at a full antimicrobial dose continued indefinitely

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
The combination of systemic amoxicillin and metronidazole (the "Van Winkelhoff" regimen), given
as a short adjunctive course alongside mechanical debridement, has the strongest evidence for
improving clinical attachment and reducing pocket depth in aggressive/Grade C periodontitis cases
associated with *A. actinomycetemcomitans*, which can persist in tissues and other intraoral
niches beyond the reach of instrumentation alone.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Chlorhexidine is a valuable local chemotherapeutic agent, but in a high-risk Grade C case
  with a specific pathogen implicated, the evidence supports an adjunctive systemic antibiotic
  course rather than relying on a topical rinse alone.
- C) A single prophylactic dose before treatment is a strategy for preventing bacteremia-related
  complications in medically compromised patients, not a therapeutic antimicrobial regimen for
  aggressive periodontitis.
- D) Long-term, full-dose antibiotic therapy is inappropriate; it promotes bacterial resistance
  and adverse effects. When doxycycline is used adjunctively, it is typically at a sub-antimicrobial
  dose for host modulation, not as a prolonged full-dose antibiotic course.
