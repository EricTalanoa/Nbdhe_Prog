# Stem
A grayish-white, filmy change on the buccal mucosa disappears when the tissue is stretched taut.
This finding is most consistent with:

# Options
- A) Leukoplakia
- B) Leukoedema (correct)
- C) Lichen planus
- D) Pseudomembranous candidiasis

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Leukoedema is a common, benign mucosal variant caused by intracellular edema of epithelial spinous
cells. Its hallmark diagnostic feature is that the grayish-white appearance diminishes or
disappears when the mucosa is stretched taut, distinguishing it from other white lesions.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) True leukoplakia is a persistent white patch, diagnosed by exclusion, that does NOT
  disappear with stretching or any other maneuver — the very fact that this lesion vanishes on
  stretch rules leukoplakia out.
- C) Lichen planus classically presents as lace-like reticular white striae (Wickham's striae),
  which persist regardless of mucosal stretching.
- D) Pseudomembranous candidiasis presents as white plaques that wipe off with gauze, leaving a
  raw, erythematous surface — a removability distinction, not a stretch-disappearance one.
