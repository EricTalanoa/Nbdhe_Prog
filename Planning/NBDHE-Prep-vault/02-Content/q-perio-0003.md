# Stem
Chlorhexidine gluconate is regarded as a gold-standard antiplaque mouthrinse largely because of its:

# Options
- A) ability to remineralize enamel
- B) substantivity — prolonged retention and slow release in the mouth (correct)
- C) complete absence of side effects
- D) selective action against only periodontal pathogens

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Chlorhexidine binds to oral surfaces (pellicle, mucosa, hydroxyapatite) and is released slowly over
several hours. This substantivity gives sustained antibacterial activity and is the key to its
efficacy.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Chlorhexidine is antimicrobial, not a remineralizing agent; that role belongs to fluoride.
- C) It has notable side effects, including extrinsic staining, altered taste, and increased calculus.
- D) It is broadly antimicrobial (gram-positive and gram-negative bacteria and yeasts), not selective.
