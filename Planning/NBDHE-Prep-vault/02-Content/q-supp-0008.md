# Stem
A soft-tissue diode laser used as an adjunct to nonsurgical periodontal therapy is appropriately
described by all of the following EXCEPT:

# Options
- A) it can aid bacterial reduction and coagulation within the pocket after scaling and root planing
- B) it is used adjunctively, in addition to, not instead of, mechanical debridement
- C) it substitutes entirely for hand and ultrasonic instrumentation and removes subgingival calculus on its own (correct)
- D) its effects depend on wavelength, power settings, and proper tissue-contact technique

# Correct answer
C

# Rationale
Soft-tissue diode lasers can supplement nonsurgical periodontal therapy — assisting with bacterial
reduction, pocket decontamination, and hemostasis — but they do not remove calculus and cannot replace
mechanical instrumentation. Effective subgingival debridement still requires hand or ultrasonic
instrumentation; the laser is an adjunct layered on top of it, and its clinical effect varies with
wavelength and operating parameters.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) True statement: adjunctive bacterial reduction and coagulation are documented benefits, so it is
  not the exception.
- B) True statement: professional guidance is explicit that laser use is adjunctive, not a replacement
  for debridement — not the exception.
- D) True statement: laser effects vary by wavelength/power/technique — not the exception.
