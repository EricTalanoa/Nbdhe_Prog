# Stem
Casein phosphopeptide-amorphous calcium phosphate (CPP-ACP) products promote remineralization of
early enamel lesions primarily by:

# Options
- A) binding calcium and phosphate ions and holding them in a bioavailable form at the tooth surface, buffering the reservoir for remineralization (correct)
- B) dissolving the outer enamel layer so new hydroxyapatite can form beneath it
- C) forming an impermeable resin coating that blocks acid contact with enamel
- D) lowering plaque pH directly to inhibit cariogenic bacteria

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
CPP, a milk-derived casein phosphopeptide, stabilizes calcium and phosphate ions in an amorphous
(non-crystalline) complex that adheres to the tooth surface and biofilm, maintaining a supersaturated
local reservoir of these ions. This bioavailable calcium/phosphate diffuses into subsurface
demineralized enamel and promotes remineralization, and CPP-ACP is often paired with fluoride for an
added anticaries effect.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) CPP-ACP does not dissolve enamel; it supplies minerals for remineralization rather than removing
  tooth structure.
- C) Unlike a pit-and-fissure sealant, CPP-ACP is not a resin barrier — it is a topically applied
  paste/cream that releases ions, not a physical coating.
- D) CPP-ACP's mechanism is mineral delivery, not direct antibacterial pH modification; that mechanism
  describes agents like xylitol or chlorhexidine, not CPP-ACP.
