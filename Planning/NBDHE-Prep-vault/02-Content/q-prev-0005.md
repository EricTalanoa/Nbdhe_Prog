# Stem
Silver diamine fluoride (SDF) arrests active carious lesions primarily through which combined
mechanism?

# Options
- A) the fluoride ion promotes remineralization while the silver ion is antibacterial (correct)
- B) the silver ion etches enamel while the fluoride ion whitens the lesion
- C) both ions act only as a physical sealant barrier over the lesion
- D) the fluoride ion is antibacterial while the silver ion promotes remineralization

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
SDF combines the antibacterial action of silver, which inhibits cariogenic bacteria such as
Streptococcus mutans, with fluoride's remineralizing effect on the demineralized tooth structure.
Together they arrest lesion progression non-invasively, at the cost of permanently staining the
treated carious tissue black.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) SDF is not an etchant, and any lightening/whitening effect is not part of its mechanism —
  in fact treated lesions darken.
- C) SDF penetrates and chemically acts on the lesion; it does not simply form a physical barrier
  the way a sealant resin does.
- D) The roles are reversed: silver (not fluoride) provides the antibacterial effect, and
  fluoride (not silver) drives remineralization.
