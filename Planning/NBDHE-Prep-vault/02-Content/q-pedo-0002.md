# Stem
For this high-caries-risk preschooler, the most appropriate professionally applied preventive agent
at this visit is:

# Options
- A) 5% sodium fluoride varnish (correct)
- B) a 1.1% sodium fluoride prescription toothpaste as the only measure
- C) pit-and-fissure sealants on the incisors
- D) no fluoride, since the community water is not fluoridated

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Fluoride varnish is safe and effective for young children — it sets on contact, limiting the amount
swallowed — and is recommended every 3-6 months for high-risk children along with caregiver
counseling and a smear of fluoride toothpaste at home.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) A 1.1% NaF prescription paste is not appropriate as the sole measure for a 4-year-old (higher
  ingestion risk), and the question asks for a professionally applied agent.
- C) Sealants are placed in the pits and fissures of posterior teeth, not on smooth incisor
  surfaces.
- D) The lack of community water fluoridation increases this child's need for topical fluoride, not
  the reverse.
