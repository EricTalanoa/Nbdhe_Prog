# Stem
Unlike sucrose, xylitol resists fermentation by cariogenic bacteria such as *Streptococcus
mutans* primarily because ______.

# Options
- A) it cannot be metabolized through the normal glycolytic pathway that produces organic acid (correct)
- B) it directly neutralizes acids already present in dental plaque
- C) it binds calcium ions to prevent enamel demineralization
- D) it inhibits salivary amylase from breaking down dietary starches

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Xylitol is a five-carbon sugar alcohol that *S. mutans* and other cariogenic bacteria cannot run
through normal glycolysis to yield organic acid end-products. Some strains take xylitol up via a
fructose PTS transporter into an energy-wasting "futile cycle" that yields no usable ATP; with
repeated exposure this can even reduce cariogenic bacterial counts. Because no acid is produced,
plaque pH does not fall the way it does after a sucrose exposure.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Xylitol's protective effect comes from *not producing* acid in the first place, not from
  buffering or neutralizing acid that bacteria have already generated.
- C) Xylitol does not chelate calcium; it has no direct mineral-binding mechanism.
- D) Xylitol has no effect on salivary amylase or starch digestion — an unrelated mechanism.
