# Stem
A cross-sectional survey finds that, at the time of screening, 45% of a community's third-graders
currently have one or more untreated carious lesions. This single-point-in-time proportion is a
measure of caries ______.

# Options
- A) incidence
- B) prevalence (correct)
- C) attack rate
- D) increment

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Prevalence is the proportion of a population that **has** a condition at a given point in time
(existing cases ÷ population). A one-time screening that counts everyone who currently has
untreated caries measures exactly that, which is why prevalence is the natural output of a
cross-sectional survey and is used to size the burden of disease when planning a program.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Incidence counts **new** cases arising over a defined period; it requires following a
  disease-free group across time, not a single snapshot, so a one-visit survey cannot yield it.
- C) Attack rate is a form of incidence used for outbreaks over a short, defined exposure period —
  again a rate of new cases, not a point-in-time proportion of existing disease.
- D) A DMFT "increment" is the change in caries experience between two examinations over time; it
  is a longitudinal difference, which a single cross-sectional survey does not provide.
