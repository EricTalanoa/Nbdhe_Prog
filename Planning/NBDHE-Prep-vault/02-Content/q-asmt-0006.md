# Stem
A patient reports well-controlled type 2 diabetes managed with oral medication and hypertension
controlled with a single antihypertensive, with no functional limitation on daily activity. Using
the ASA Physical Status Classification System, this patient is best classified as:

# Options
- A) ASA I
- B) ASA II (correct)
- C) ASA III
- D) ASA IV

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
ASA II describes a patient with mild-to-moderate systemic disease that is well controlled and
does not limit activity, such as controlled diabetes or controlled hypertension. This
classification signals that routine dental hygiene care can typically proceed with standard
precautions and monitoring.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) ASA I is a completely healthy patient with no systemic disease; this patient has two
  managed conditions.
- C) ASA III describes severe systemic disease that limits activity (e.g., poorly controlled
  diabetes, angina); this patient's conditions are controlled and non-limiting.
- D) ASA IV describes severe systemic disease that is a constant threat to life; that is far more
  severe than well-controlled diabetes and hypertension.
