# Stem
A patient declines the recommended periodontal treatment after the risks of forgoing it were
explained. To manage risk, the hygienist should document this as an:

# Options
- A) informed refusal, noting the risks disclosed and the patient's decision to decline (correct)
- B) informed consent, since the conversation about risks took place
- C) incident report, since the patient did not follow the treatment plan
- D) amendment to the treatment plan showing the procedure as completed

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Informed refusal documents that the patient was informed of the risks of not proceeding with
recommended care and still chose to decline it. This record protects both patient and clinician by
showing the discussion occurred and the decision was the patient's autonomous choice.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Informed consent documents agreement to proceed with treatment, not a decision to decline it.
- C) An incident report documents an adverse event or safety issue, not a patient's informed
  treatment decision.
- D) The treatment was not completed, so charting it as completed would be inaccurate and could
  constitute fraudulent documentation.
