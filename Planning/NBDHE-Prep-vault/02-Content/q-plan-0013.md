# Stem
When presenting the dental hygiene care plan to a patient, all of the following are appropriate
EXCEPT:

# Options
- A) explaining findings in language the patient can understand, avoiding technical jargon
- B) describing the risks, benefits, and alternatives to the proposed treatment
- C) pressuring the patient to accept the full plan in a single visit to avoid disease progression (correct)
- D) inviting the patient to ask questions and voice concerns before obtaining consent

# Correct answer
C

# Rationale
Case presentation should support the patient's autonomous, informed decision-making. Pressuring a
patient to accept care undermines informed consent, which must be given voluntarily and free of
coercion, even when delaying treatment carries some clinical risk that should instead be explained
clearly.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Using plain language so the patient understands findings is a core element of effective case
  presentation.
- B) Disclosing risks, benefits, and alternatives is required for legally and ethically valid
  informed consent.
- D) Inviting questions and addressing concerns is appropriate and supports genuine informed
  consent.
