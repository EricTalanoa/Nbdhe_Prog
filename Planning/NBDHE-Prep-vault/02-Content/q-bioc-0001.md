# Stem
A deficiency of vitamin C impairs collagen synthesis and classically produces which oral finding?

# Options
- A) swollen, spongy gingiva that bleeds easily (correct)
- B) enamel hypoplasia of the developing teeth
- C) angular cheilitis at the corners of the mouth
- D) a smooth, red, atrophic tongue

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Vitamin C (ascorbic acid) is a cofactor for the hydroxylation of proline and lysine during collagen
synthesis. Deficiency (scurvy) weakens connective tissue and vessel walls, producing swollen,
spongy gingiva that bleeds readily.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Enamel hypoplasia arises from disturbances during enamel formation (e.g., febrile illness,
  fluorosis, vitamin A/D issues), not vitamin C deficiency.
- C) Angular cheilitis is associated with B-complex (riboflavin) deficiency and Candida.
- D) A smooth, atrophic red tongue is linked to vitamin B12, folate, or iron deficiency.
