# Stem
The primary purpose of a personnel dosimeter (film badge) worn by a dental radiographer is to:

# Options
- A) reduce the radiation dose received by the operator
- B) measure and record the cumulative occupational radiation exposure of the wearer (correct)
- C) shield the operator's thyroid from scatter radiation
- D) filter out low-energy photons before they reach the patient

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
A personnel dosimeter does not protect the wearer; it is a monitoring device that measures and
records the cumulative radiation dose the operator receives over a defined period, allowing
comparison against occupational exposure limits and early detection of unsafe practices or
equipment malfunction.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) The dosimeter is a passive recording device; it does not attenuate or reduce the dose the
  operator actually receives.
- C) A thyroid collar, not a dosimeter, shields the thyroid; the dosimeter is typically worn at
  collar level or on clothing to sample exposure, not to protect an organ.
- D) Filtration to remove low-energy photons is a function of the X-ray tube's aluminum filter, not
  the personnel dosimeter.
