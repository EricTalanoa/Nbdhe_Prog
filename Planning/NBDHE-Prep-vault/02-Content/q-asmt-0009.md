# Stem
When examining the temporomandibular joint, all of the following are appropriate assessment
techniques EXCEPT:

# Options
- A) palpating the joint bilaterally during opening and closing for clicking or crepitus
- B) measuring the maximum interincisal opening in millimeters
- C) observing the pattern of mandibular movement for deviation on opening
- D) injecting local anesthetic into the joint space to rule out internal derangement (correct)

# Correct answer
D

# Rationale
This is a negative-format item — the key is the technique that is NOT an appropriate part of a
dental hygiene TMJ screening. Injecting anesthetic into the joint space is an invasive diagnostic
or therapeutic procedure outside the scope of a hygiene examination, not a screening technique.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Bilateral palpation for clicking, popping, or crepitus is a standard, noninvasive TMJ
  screening step.
- B) Measuring interincisal opening quantifies range of motion and is a routine part of the exam.
- C) Observing for deviation or deflection during opening is a standard noninvasive assessment.
