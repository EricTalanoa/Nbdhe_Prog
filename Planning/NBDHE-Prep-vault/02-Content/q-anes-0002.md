# Stem
Amide local anesthetics such as lidocaine are biotransformed primarily in the:

# Options
- A) plasma, by pseudocholinesterase
- B) liver (correct)
- C) kidney
- D) lungs

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Amide anesthetics (lidocaine, mepivacaine, prilocaine, bupivacaine) are metabolized mainly in the
liver, so significant hepatic dysfunction can prolong their action and raise toxicity risk.
Articaine is a partial exception — it is largely hydrolyzed by plasma esterases.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Plasma pseudocholinesterase hydrolyzes *ester* anesthetics (e.g., procaine), not amides.
- C) The kidney excretes metabolites but is not the primary site of biotransformation.
- D) The lungs can take up some drug but are not the main metabolic organ for amides.
