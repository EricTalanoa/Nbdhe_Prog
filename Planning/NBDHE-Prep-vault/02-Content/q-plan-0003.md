# Stem
All of the following practices are consistent with CDC-recommended standard precautions in the
treatment room EXCEPT:

# Options
- A) performing hand hygiene and donning a new pair of gloves before treating each patient
- B) using high-volume evacuation and a rubber dam, when feasible, to reduce aerosol and spatter
- C) recapping a contaminated local anesthetic needle by hand using a two-handed technique (correct)
- D) placing contaminated instruments in a rigid, leak-proof, labeled container for transport to sterilization

# Correct answer
C

# Rationale
Two-handed needle recapping requires guiding the needle into the cap by hand, which is a leading
cause of accidental needlestick injury. CDC and OSHA guidance calls for either a one-handed
"scoop" technique or a mechanical recapping device so the clinician's free hand never approaches
the exposed needle.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) New gloves and hand hygiene for every patient are a core standard-precautions requirement.
- B) A rubber dam plus high-volume evacuation is a recommended engineering control for reducing
  aerosol and spatter exposure.
- D) Rigid, labeled, leak-proof transport containers are required for safely moving contaminated
  instruments to sterilization.
