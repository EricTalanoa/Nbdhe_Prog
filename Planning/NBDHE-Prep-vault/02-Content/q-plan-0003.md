# Stem
A patient presents with generalized moderate chronic periodontitis and heavy supragingival
calculus, along with two carious lesions noted by the dentist. When sequencing the dental
hygiene care plan, the hygienist should generally:

# Options
- A) complete restorative treatment first so a clean field is available for periodontal therapy
- B) provide disease-control therapy (oral hygiene instruction, scaling) before definitive periodontal therapy, then reevaluate (correct)
- C) place the patient directly into the periodontal maintenance phase
- D) sequence purely by tooth number, alternating quadrants regardless of disease severity

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Standard care-plan sequencing places a disease-control (Phase I) step first: patient education,
scaling, and removal of local irritants to reduce inflammation and reinforce home care, followed
by reevaluation before any definitive or surgical periodontal therapy is planned. This lets the
tissue response guide the rest of the plan.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Restorative treatment is typically deferred until inflammation is controlled and caries risk
  is managed; operating on inflamed tissue first is not the standard sequence.
- C) Maintenance follows active therapy and reevaluation of a stable, treated patient — it is not
  the entry point for a patient with active disease.
- D) Sequencing must be driven by disease severity and patient needs, not an arbitrary tooth-number
  rotation.
