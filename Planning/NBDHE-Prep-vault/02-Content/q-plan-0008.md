# Stem
Which behavior-management technique reduces a patient's anxiety by demonstrating a procedure on a
model or in a non-threatening way before performing it on the patient?

# Options
- A) Tell-show-do (correct)
- B) Systematic desensitization
- C) Positive reinforcement
- D) Voice control

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Tell-show-do explains the procedure in age-appropriate language, demonstrates it on a model or
inanimate object, and then performs it on the patient, reducing fear of the unknown.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Systematic desensitization gradually exposes the patient to progressively more anxiety-provoking
  stimuli over multiple visits; it does not center on a single demonstration step.
- C) Positive reinforcement rewards desired behavior after it occurs; it is not a pre-procedure
  demonstration technique.
- D) Voice control uses tone and firmness to gain a patient's attention and compliance; it does not
  involve demonstrating the procedure.
