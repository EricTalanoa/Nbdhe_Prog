# Stem
According to the Spaulding classification used to guide dental instrument processing, a mouth
mirror that contacts only intact oral mucosa is classified as:

# Options
- A) semicritical, requiring at minimum high-level disinfection (correct)
- B) critical, requiring sterilization only, since any instrument used intraorally must be sterilized
- C) noncritical, requiring only surface wiping with a low-level disinfectant
- D) exempt from processing between patients because it does not penetrate soft tissue or bone

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Semicritical items contact mucous membranes or non-intact skin but do not penetrate soft tissue,
bone, or the bloodstream. CDC guidance sets high-level disinfection as the minimum standard for
these items, though heat-stable semicritical items such as most metal mirrors are routinely
heat-sterilized in practice for an added margin of safety.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Critical items penetrate soft tissue, bone, or enter the bloodstream (e.g., scalers,
  extraction forceps) and require sterilization; contacting only mucosa does not meet that
  definition.
- C) Noncritical items contact only intact skin (e.g., a blood pressure cuff, the dental chair); a
  mirror used intraorally touches mucosa, a higher-risk category than that.
- D) All patient-care items require appropriate processing between patients regardless of whether
  they penetrate tissue.
