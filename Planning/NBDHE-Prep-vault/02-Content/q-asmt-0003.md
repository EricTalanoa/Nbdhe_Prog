# Stem
On palpation, a cervical lymph node that is hard, fixed to surrounding tissue, and non-tender is
most concerning for:

# Options
- A) malignancy (correct)
- B) an acute bacterial infection
- C) a normal, healthy node
- D) a seasonal allergic response

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Nodes involved by malignancy classically feel firm to hard, become fixed to adjacent tissues, and
are typically non-tender. These features warrant prompt referral for evaluation.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Nodes reacting to acute infection are usually tender, soft to firm, and freely movable.
- C) Normal nodes are often non-palpable or small, soft, and mobile.
- D) An allergic response would not produce a hard, fixed, non-tender node.
