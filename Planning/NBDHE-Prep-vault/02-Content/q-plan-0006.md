# Stem
For a caries-active patient, the dietary counseling point most directly tied to lowering caries risk
is to reduce the:

# Options
- A) frequency of fermentable carbohydrate intake between meals (correct)
- B) total daily volume of water consumed
- C) intake of dietary calcium below the RDA
- D) number of times per day the teeth are brushed

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Each exposure to fermentable carbohydrate drops plaque pH below the critical level for roughly 20+
minutes. Caries risk therefore tracks with how *often* teeth are challenged, so cutting the
frequency of between-meal sugars is the highest-yield dietary change.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Water intake is not a caries driver (and adequate hydration supports salivary flow).
- C) Lowering calcium would not help; it is not the modifiable caries factor here.
- D) Brushing more relates to biofilm removal, not diet; the question targets dietary counseling,
  and one would not advise brushing *less*.
