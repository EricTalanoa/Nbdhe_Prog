# Stem
Which microorganism is most strongly associated with the initiation of dental caries?

# Options
- A) Porphyromonas gingivalis
- B) Streptococcus mutans (correct)
- C) Aggregatibacter actinomycetemcomitans
- D) Lactobacillus acidophilus

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Streptococcus mutans is the principal initiator of caries: it adheres to the acquired pellicle,
synthesizes extracellular polysaccharides that build biofilm, and is highly acidogenic and aciduric,
demineralizing enamel.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Porphyromonas gingivalis is a key periodontal ("red complex") pathogen, not a caries initiator.
- C) Aggregatibacter actinomycetemcomitans is associated with localized aggressive periodontitis.
- D) Lactobacillus species are linked to the *progression* of established lesions rather than
  initiation.
