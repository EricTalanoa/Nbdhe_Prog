# Stem
The "certainly lethal dose" of fluoride is best described as:

# Options
- A) the smallest dose that produces any observable toxic symptoms
- B) the dose at which about half of an exposed population would be expected to die
- C) the lowest dose reported to have caused death in a human, without emergency treatment (correct)
- D) the maximum daily dose considered safe for long-term ingestion

# Correct answer
C

# Rationale
The "certainly lethal dose" (CLD) is the lowest reported dose that has caused death in a human
without treatment; for fluoride it is roughly 32-64 mg F per kg body weight, and it is the
benchmark used to decide whether emergency hospital referral is needed after an acute ingestion.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) That describes a toxic (not lethal) threshold, sometimes called the "safely tolerated dose"
  boundary, a much smaller amount that causes GI upset, not death.
- B) A dose lethal to 50% of a population (an LD50) is a toxicology concept typically derived from
  animal studies, not the clinical CLD benchmark used for human fluoride emergencies.
- D) The adequate/safe daily intake for ongoing exposure is an entirely different concept from a
  single acute lethal dose.
