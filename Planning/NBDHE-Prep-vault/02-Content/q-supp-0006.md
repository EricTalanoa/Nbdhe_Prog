# Stem
All of the following will shorten the setting time of an alginate impression material EXCEPT:

# Options
- A) using cooler water in the mix (correct)
- B) using warmer water in the mix
- C) increasing the powder-to-water ratio
- D) adding excess retarder-free accelerator (e.g., a "fast-set" formula)

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Alginate's gelation reaction (calcium sulfate reacting with sodium/potassium alginate to form
calcium alginate) is temperature-dependent: cooler water slows the reaction and lengthens working
and setting time, the opposite of shortening it.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Warmer water speeds the chemical reaction and shortens setting time.
- C) A higher powder-to-water ratio (thicker mix) sets faster than the manufacturer's recommended
  ratio.
- D) Fast-set formulations are manufactured with accelerators specifically to shorten setting time.
