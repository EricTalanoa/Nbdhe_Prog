# Stem
Exceeding the maximum dose of which local anesthetic is most likely to precipitate
methemoglobinemia?

# Options
- A) prilocaine (correct)
- B) lidocaine
- C) mepivacaine
- D) bupivacaine

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Prilocaine is metabolized to o-toluidine, which oxidizes the iron in hemoglobin from the ferrous to
the ferric state, forming methemoglobin that cannot carry oxygen. At high doses this can produce
clinically significant methemoglobinemia (cyanosis unresponsive to oxygen). Topical benzocaine is
another well-known cause.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Lidocaine is not a notable cause of methemoglobinemia at clinical doses.
- C) Mepivacaine does not carry a meaningful methemoglobinemia risk.
- D) Bupivacaine is not associated with methemoglobinemia.
