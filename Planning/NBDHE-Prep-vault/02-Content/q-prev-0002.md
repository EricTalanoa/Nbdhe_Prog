# Stem
A dental sealant is most appropriately indicated for a:

# Options
- A) tooth with frank cavitation extending into dentin
- B) caries-free but deep, retentive occlusal pit or fissure at risk of caries (correct)
- C) smooth interproximal surface
- D) tooth already restored with a full-coverage crown

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Sealants are indicated for sound or non-cavitated pit-and-fissure surfaces that are deep and
retentive and therefore at risk. The sealant physically occludes the fissure, denying bacteria and
fermentable carbohydrate access to the vulnerable surface.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Frank cavitation into dentin requires a restoration, not a sealant.
- C) Sealants are placed on pits and fissures, not smooth interproximal surfaces (managed with
  fluoride and interdental cleaning).
- D) A crowned tooth has no exposed occlusal fissure system to seal.
