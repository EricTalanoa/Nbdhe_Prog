# Stem
A vasoconstrictor is added to a local anesthetic solution primarily to:

# Options
- A) increase the pH of the solution
- B) slow systemic absorption of the anesthetic (correct)
- C) speed the onset of pulpal anesthesia
- D) prevent an allergic reaction to the anesthetic

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Vasoconstrictors (e.g., epinephrine) constrict local blood vessels, which slows the rate at which
the anesthetic is carried away from the injection site. This prolongs duration, deepens the block,
lowers peak plasma levels (improving safety), and reduces bleeding in the field.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Vasoconstrictor-containing solutions are actually more acidic (they include an antioxidant
  such as sodium metabisulfite), which tends to *slow* onset — not a pH-raising benefit.
- C) Onset is governed mainly by the anesthetic's pKa and lipid solubility and the tissue pH, not
  by the vasoconstrictor; if anything the more acidic solution can delay onset slightly.
- D) Vasoconstrictors do not prevent allergy; the metabisulfite preservative is itself a more
  common trigger of sensitivity reactions.
