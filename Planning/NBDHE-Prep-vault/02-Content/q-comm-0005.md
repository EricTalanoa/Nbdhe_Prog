# Stem
A school-based program applies pit-and-fissure sealants to the permanent molars of caries-free
second-graders. This intervention is best classified as which level of prevention?

# Options
- A) primary prevention (correct)
- B) secondary prevention
- C) tertiary prevention
- D) quaternary prevention

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Primary prevention keeps disease from occurring in the first place by acting **before** any
pathology is present. Sealing sound, caries-free occlusal surfaces physically blocks plaque and
fermentable carbohydrate from the deep pits and fissures, preventing new lesions — a textbook
primary-prevention measure, alongside community water fluoridation and health education.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Secondary prevention aims at early detection and treatment of disease that has **already
  begun** — for example, screening surveys or restoring an incipient lesion before it progresses.
  The children here are caries-free, so there is no early disease to detect.
- C) Tertiary prevention limits disability and restores function **after** disease has caused
  damage — such as prosthetic rehabilitation of a patient who has lost teeth. That does not apply
  to sound teeth.
- D) "Quaternary prevention" (avoiding over-medicalization) is not one of the three classic public
  health levels tested here and does not describe placing a preventive sealant.
