# Stem
Selective (need-based) polishing is recommended primarily because routine coronal polishing:

# Options
- A) takes too much appointment time to justify
- B) can abrade the fluoride-rich outer enamel and exposed cementum without a therapeutic benefit (correct)
- C) always causes gingival bleeding
- D) blocks the uptake of fluoride applied afterward

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Fluoride concentration is highest in the outermost enamel, and abrasive prophylaxis paste can remove
a thin layer of that surface as well as scratch exposed cementum, dentin, and esthetic restorations.
Because polishing is cosmetic (stain removal) rather than therapeutic, it should be done selectively,
only where extrinsic stain is present.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Time is not the main rationale; the concern is unnecessary abrasion of tooth structure and
  restorations.
- C) Polishing does not always cause bleeding.
- D) Polishing does not prevent later fluoride uptake — that is a myth; the real concern is loss of
  surface enamel.
