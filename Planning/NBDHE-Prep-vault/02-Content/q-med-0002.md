# Stem
Which laboratory value best reflects this warfarin patient's bleeding risk before periodontal
instrumentation?

# Options
- A) International Normalized Ratio (INR) (correct)
- B) fasting blood glucose
- C) white blood cell count
- D) resting heart rate

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Warfarin inhibits vitamin K-dependent clotting factors, and its effect is monitored by the
prothrombin time expressed as the INR. A higher INR indicates greater anticoagulation and bleeding
risk; the therapeutic range for atrial fibrillation is generally about 2.0-3.0.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Fasting blood glucose reflects diabetic control, not anticoagulation.
- C) White blood cell count reflects infection or immune status, not clotting.
- D) Resting heart rate is relevant to her atrial fibrillation but is not a measure of coagulation
  or bleeding risk.
