# Stem
Vitamin D supports mineralization of bone and teeth primarily by:

# Options
- A) increasing intestinal absorption of calcium and phosphate (correct)
- B) directly inhibiting the formation of osteoclasts
- C) acting as a cofactor for collagen cross-linking
- D) neutralizing acids produced by plaque bacteria

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Active vitamin D (calcitriol) increases intestinal absorption of calcium and phosphate, raising
their plasma concentrations so that bone and tooth matrix can mineralize.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Vitamin D supports normal bone remodeling and, if anything, mobilizes calcium; it does not
  simply inhibit osteoclasts.
- C) Collagen cross-linking depends on vitamin C and copper-dependent lysyl oxidase, not vitamin D.
- D) Acid neutralization in the mouth is a buffering function of saliva, unrelated to vitamin D.
