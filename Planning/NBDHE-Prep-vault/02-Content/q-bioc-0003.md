# Stem
A deficiency of which vitamin impairs collagen synthesis by preventing the hydroxylation of
proline and lysine, classically presenting with spongy, bleeding gingiva?

# Options
- A) vitamin C (ascorbic acid) (correct)
- B) vitamin D
- C) vitamin K
- D) vitamin B12

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Vitamin C is a required cofactor for prolyl and lysyl hydroxylase, the enzymes that stabilize
collagen's triple helix. Deficiency (scurvy) produces weak, friable connective tissue, spongy and
bleeding gingiva, and poor wound healing.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Vitamin D regulates calcium and phosphate absorption and bone mineralization; it is not a
  cofactor for collagen hydroxylation.
- C) Vitamin K is required to synthesize clotting factors II, VII, IX, and X; its deficiency
  causes a coagulopathy, not a collagen hydroxylation defect.
- D) Vitamin B12 is needed for DNA synthesis and red blood cell maturation; deficiency causes
  glossitis and megaloblastic anemia, not impaired collagen hydroxylation.
