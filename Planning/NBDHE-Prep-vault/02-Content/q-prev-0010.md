# Stem
A patient has full-coverage porcelain crowns on the maxillary anterior teeth and several composite
resin restorations. Which topical fluoride is LEAST appropriate for this patient's in-office
fluoride treatment, due to its potential to etch or dull esthetic restorative surfaces?

# Options
- A) Acidulated phosphate fluoride (APF) gel (correct)
- B) Neutral sodium fluoride (NaF) gel
- C) Stannous fluoride varnish
- D) Silver diamine fluoride (SDF)

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
APF's acidic pH is what makes it effective at enhancing fluorapatite uptake on enamel, but that
same acidity etches and dulls the glaze on porcelain and roughens composite and glass ionomer
restorative surfaces with repeated exposure. Neutral sodium fluoride avoids this problem and is
the preferred choice for patients with extensive esthetic restorations.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Neutral NaF's near-neutral pH does not etch restorative surfaces, making it the appropriate
  substitute — the opposite of what this question asks for.
- C) Fluoride varnishes set on contact with saliva and are not associated with etching esthetic
  restorations the way an acidic gel is.
- D) SDF is a distinct minimally invasive caries-arrest agent, not the topical fluoride agent
  implicated in this esthetic-restoration etching concern.
