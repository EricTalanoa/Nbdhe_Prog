# Stem
A patient's dental implant shows erythema and bleeding on gentle probing around the peri-implant
mucosa, but the most recent radiograph shows no change in crestal bone level compared with the
baseline film taken at restoration. This presentation is most consistent with:

# Options
- A) peri-implant mucositis (correct)
- B) peri-implantitis
- C) implant failure from occlusal overload
- D) normal peri-implant tissue

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Peri-implant mucositis is inflammation confined to the soft tissue around an implant — bleeding
and/or suppuration on probing — with no accompanying loss of supporting bone. It is the
peri-implant analog of gingivitis and is reversible with improved biofilm control.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Peri-implantitis requires both soft-tissue inflammation and progressive radiographic bone
  loss beyond initial remodeling; bone level here is unchanged from baseline.
- C) Occlusal overload presents with progressive bone loss and possible implant mobility, not
  isolated soft-tissue bleeding with a stable radiograph.
- D) Bleeding on probing is an inflammatory sign, not a normal finding.
