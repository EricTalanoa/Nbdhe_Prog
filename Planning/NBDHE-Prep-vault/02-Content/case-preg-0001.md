# Demographics
Female, age 28. 24 weeks pregnant (second trimester), first pregnancy, uncomplicated to date.
Healthy; takes only a prenatal vitamin. No known drug allergies. Blood pressure 118/74.

# Chief Complaint
"My gums have gotten really puffy and they bleed a lot since I got pregnant."

# Background / History
Last professional cleaning was about 10 months ago. Reports first-trimester nausea made
toothbrushing difficult and she skipped it some mornings. Brushes twice daily now with a manual
brush; flosses "once in a while." Diet includes frequent between-meal snacking on crackers and
juice to manage nausea. Nonsmoker; no alcohol during pregnancy. Obstetrician has cleared her for
routine dental care.

# Current Findings
Generalized marginal gingival erythema and edema with rolled, glossy margins and generalized
bleeding on probing. Probing depths are mostly 3 mm, with pseudopockets to 4 mm in the posterior
where the swollen tissue rides higher on the crown; **no clinical attachment loss** and no
radiographic bone loss. A soft, red, pedunculated interproximal gingival overgrowth (~6 mm) is
present between #8 and #9, bleeds readily, and is not painful. Generalized moderate plaque
biofilm; light supragingival calculus on the mandibular anteriors.

# Notes
Original case authored to the blueprint (Rule 0) — no real patient or exam data. Two linked items
(q-preg-0001, q-preg-0002) draw on this patient box. The #8–9 lesion is described as a reactive
pregnancy-associated pyogenic granuloma ("pregnancy epulis"); the case is built so the exaggerated
gingival response sits on top of a plaque etiology, not a spontaneous one.
