# Stem
A generally healthy adolescent presents with a localized pattern of rapid, severe attachment loss
around the first molars and incisors, with relatively little plaque and calculus present. Which
microorganism is most strongly associated with this specific presentation?

# Options
- A) Aggregatibacter actinomycetemcomitans (correct)
- B) Porphyromonas gingivalis
- C) Streptococcus mutans
- D) Candida albicans

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Aggregatibacter actinomycetemcomitans, a leukotoxin-producing organism, is classically associated
with localized aggressive (Grade C, molar-incisor pattern) periodontitis in otherwise healthy
adolescents — rapid attachment loss disproportionate to the modest local plaque/calculus present.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Porphyromonas gingivalis is a red-complex organism more strongly linked to chronic,
  generalized periodontitis in adults with heavier biofilm and calculus accumulation, not this
  localized adolescent-onset pattern.
- C) Streptococcus mutans is the primary cariogenic organism responsible for dental caries, not a
  periodontal pathogen tied to this presentation.
- D) Candida albicans is an opportunistic fungal organism associated with conditions like denture
  stomatitis, not this periodontal presentation.
