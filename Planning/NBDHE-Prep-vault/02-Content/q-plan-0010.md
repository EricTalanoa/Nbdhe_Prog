# Stem
When planning an individualized oral hygiene instruction sequence for a new patient, the dental
hygienist's first step should be to:

# Options
- A) demonstrate the recommended brushing and flossing technique on a model
- B) assess the patient's current knowledge, motivation, and manual dexterity (correct)
- C) select the specific interdental aid the patient will be assigned
- D) schedule a follow-up appointment to evaluate compliance

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Individualized instruction begins with an assessment of the patient's baseline knowledge,
readiness/motivation to change, and physical ability to perform oral self-care. This assessment
determines which techniques and aids are realistic and how the message should be framed, so it
must precede any demonstration, product selection, or follow-up planning.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Demonstrating a technique before understanding the patient's needs and abilities risks
  teaching a method the patient cannot perform or is not motivated to use.
- C) Choosing a specific aid is a downstream decision that should be based on the assessment, not
  the starting point.
- D) Evaluating compliance is a later step in the education cycle, after instruction has been
  delivered and practiced.
