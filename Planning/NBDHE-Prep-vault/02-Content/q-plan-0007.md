# Stem
The most important message to give a patient newly diagnosed with plaque-induced gingivitis is that
the condition is:

# Options
- A) reversible with improved daily biofilm removal (correct)
- B) irreversible and will inevitably progress to periodontitis
- C) unrelated to daily oral hygiene practices
- D) treatable only with systemic antibiotics

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Gingivitis is a reversible inflammatory response to bacterial biofilm at the gingival margin.
Emphasizing that consistent, effective daily plaque removal can restore gingival health motivates
the patient and sets realistic, achievable expectations.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Gingivitis does not inevitably progress; with adequate biofilm control it resolves without
  attachment loss.
- C) Gingivitis is directly caused by bacterial biofilm accumulation, so oral hygiene is central to
  both cause and resolution.
- D) Mechanical biofilm removal, not systemic antibiotics, is the standard first-line management for
  plaque-induced gingivitis.
