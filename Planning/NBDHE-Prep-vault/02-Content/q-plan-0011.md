# Stem
A patient taking multiple medications reports a persistently dry mouth. Along with recommending
frequent water intake and sugar-free xylitol gum, the most appropriate additional instruction is
to:

# Options
- A) discontinue the medications causing the dryness
- B) increase the frequency of professional fluoride application and reduce cariogenic snacking (correct)
- C) switch to an alcohol-containing mouthrinse to freshen breath
- D) reassure the patient that xerostomia carries no additional oral health risk

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Xerostomia reduces the buffering, remineralizing, and antimicrobial protection normally provided
by saliva, sharply raising caries risk. Patient education should include more frequent
professional and home fluoride use plus reducing between-meal fermentable carbohydrate exposure,
alongside salivary stimulants/substitutes.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) The hygienist cannot advise a patient to stop a prescribed medication; that decision belongs
  to the prescribing provider.
- C) Alcohol-containing rinses are drying and can worsen xerostomia-related discomfort.
- D) Xerostomia significantly increases caries and mucosal risk, so it must not be dismissed.
