# Stem
Which structure induces root dentin formation and, after fragmenting, allows cementoblasts to
differentiate and deposit cementum on the newly formed root surface?

# Options
- A) dental follicle
- B) stellate reticulum
- C) Hertwig's epithelial root sheath (correct)
- D) dental papilla

# Correct answer
C

# Rationale
Hertwig's epithelial root sheath (HERS), a proliferation of the cervical loop's inner and outer
enamel epithelium, maps out root shape and induces the adjacent dental papilla cells to become
root-forming odontoblasts. As HERS fragments into the epithelial rests of Malassez, dental follicle
cells contact the exposed root dentin, differentiate into cementoblasts, and begin cementogenesis.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) The dental follicle supplies the cementoblasts, cementum, periodontal ligament, and alveolar
  bone, but it is HERS that triggers root dentin formation and must fragment first.
- B) The stellate reticulum is a crown-stage enamel organ layer that supports ameloblast nutrition;
  it has no role in root formation.
- D) The dental papilla gives rise to odontoblasts and pulp, but its root-forming odontoblasts are
  induced by HERS rather than the papilla acting independently.
