# Stem
When applying research findings to an individual patient's care, evidence-based decision making
requires the clinician to integrate the best available scientific evidence with which other two
components?

# Options
- A) the clinician's professional expertise and the patient's needs, values, and preferences (correct)
- B) the manufacturer's marketing claims and the clinic's supply budget
- C) the most recently published study only and office scheduling constraints
- D) national practice averages and insurance reimbursement policy

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Evidence-based decision making is defined as a three-part integration: the best available
scientific/clinical evidence, the clinician's own expertise and clinical judgment, and the individual
patient's needs, values, and preferences. Applying research results well means weighing all three, not
adopting a study's conclusion in isolation.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Marketing claims and budget are not part of the evidence-based triad and are not appropriate
  substitutes for clinical evidence or patient-centered judgment.
- C) A single recent study, without appraisal or context, and scheduling logistics do not represent the
  triad's clinician-expertise or patient-preference components.
- D) Population-level averages and reimbursement policy may inform decisions but are not the defined
  components of evidence-based decision making.
