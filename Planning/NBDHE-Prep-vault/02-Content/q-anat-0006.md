# Stem
The mandibular first molar most commonly presents with which root configuration?

# Options
- A) a single fused root with two canals
- B) three roots: mesiobuccal, distobuccal, and palatal
- C) two roots: mesial and distal, each with two canals typical on the mesial root (correct)
- D) two roots: buccal and lingual

# Correct answer
C

# Rationale
The mandibular first molar typically has two roots — a broader mesial root and a narrower distal
root — separated by a furcation on the buccal, lingual, and interradicular surfaces. The mesial
root very commonly contains two canals (mesiobuccal and mesiolingual), while the distal root more
often has a single, wider canal.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) A single fused root is atypical for a mandibular first molar and is more characteristic of some
  mandibular premolars or fused/taurodontic anomalies.
- B) A mesiobuccal/distobuccal/palatal three-root pattern describes the maxillary first molar, not
  a mandibular molar.
- D) A buccal/lingual root division does not describe molar anatomy; mandibular molar roots divide
  mesial and distal, not buccal and lingual.
