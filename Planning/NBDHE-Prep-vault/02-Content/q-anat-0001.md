# Stem
The inferior alveolar nerve enters the mandible through which opening?

# Options
- A) mental foramen
- B) mandibular foramen (correct)
- C) greater palatine foramen
- D) infraorbital foramen

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
The inferior alveolar nerve enters the mandibular canal through the mandibular foramen on the
medial surface of the ramus, then travels forward to supply the mandibular posterior teeth. It
exits anteriorly at the mental foramen as the mental nerve.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) The mental foramen is where the nerve *exits* the mandible, not where it enters.
- C) The greater palatine foramen transmits the greater palatine nerve to the hard palate (maxilla).
- D) The infraorbital foramen transmits the infraorbital nerve, a maxillary structure.
