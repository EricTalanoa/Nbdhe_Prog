# Stem
Foreshortening — teeth appearing shorter than they actually are on a periapical radiograph —
results from:

# Options
- A) excessive vertical angulation of the beam (correct)
- B) insufficient vertical angulation of the beam
- C) incorrect horizontal angulation of the beam
- D) placing the receptor too far from the tooth

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
With the bisecting-angle technique, too much vertical angulation directs the beam more
perpendicular to the receptor than to the tooth's long axis, projecting a shortened
(foreshortened) image.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Insufficient vertical angulation causes the opposite error — elongation (images too long).
- C) Incorrect horizontal angulation produces overlapping of the proximal contacts, not a length
  error.
- D) Increasing receptor-to-tooth distance magnifies the image; it does not cause foreshortening.
