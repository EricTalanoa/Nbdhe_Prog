# Stem
Small, painless, yellow-white papules noted on the buccal mucosa and the vermilion border of the
lips in an otherwise healthy adult most likely represent:

# Options
- A) Fordyce granules (correct)
- B) Squamous cell carcinoma
- C) Pseudomembranous candidiasis
- D) Recurrent herpetic vesicles

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Fordyce granules are ectopic sebaceous glands, a common benign developmental variant that appears
as clusters of small, painless yellow-white papules, most often on the buccal mucosa and lip
vermilion. They require no treatment and are simply documented as a normal finding.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Squamous cell carcinoma typically presents as an indurated ulcer or an erythroleukoplakic
  lesion, not as painless yellow papules, and it is not a normal anatomic variant.
- C) Pseudomembranous candidiasis presents as removable white, curd-like plaques (or erythema),
  a very different clinical appearance from discrete yellow papules.
- D) Herpetic vesicles are painful, fluid-filled lesions that rupture into ulcers — an acute,
  symptomatic presentation unlike the asymptomatic finding described.
