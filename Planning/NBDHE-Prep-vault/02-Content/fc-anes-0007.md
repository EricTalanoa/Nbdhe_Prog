# Front
What local anesthetic property correlates most closely with duration of action, and why?

# Back
Protein binding. Local anesthetics act by binding to the sodium channel protein; the more
tightly a drug binds (higher % protein-bound, e.g., bupivacaine ~95%), the more slowly it
releases from the channel, giving a longer clinical duration. This is separate from lipid
solubility (governs potency) and pKa (governs onset speed).
