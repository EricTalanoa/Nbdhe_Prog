# Stem
Immediately after a small blood vessel is cut during scaling, which event occurs **first** in the
sequence of hemostasis?

# Options
- A) vascular spasm (vasoconstriction) of the injured vessel (correct)
- B) formation of a stable fibrin mesh
- C) clot retraction
- D) fibrinolysis

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Hemostasis proceeds in an ordered sequence: (1) **vascular spasm** — the injured vessel constricts
almost instantly via local myogenic reflex and released mediators, reducing blood flow; (2) a
**platelet plug** forms as platelets adhere and aggregate at the exposed collagen; (3) **coagulation**
lays down a fibrin mesh that stabilizes the plug; then later clot retraction and, eventually,
fibrinolysis. Vasoconstriction is the first, near-immediate response.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Fibrin formation is the coagulation-cascade step that comes **after** the platelet plug — it
  reinforces an already-forming clot, not the first response.
- C) Clot retraction pulls the edges of an existing clot together to close the wound; it happens
  well after the clot has formed.
- D) Fibrinolysis is the breakdown of the clot once healing is underway — the opposite of an
  initial hemostatic response.
