# Demographics
Male, age 54. Type 2 diabetes mellitus, most recent HbA1c 8.5% (patient states he "hasn't checked
sugars in months"). Hypertension. Current smoker, ~20 cigarettes/day for 30 years (30 pack-years).
No known drug allergies.

# Chief Complaint
"A couple of my teeth feel loose and my breath is bad. I haven't seen a dentist in about four years."

# Background / History
Medications: metformin and amlodipine. Reports a persistently dry mouth. Diet includes frequent
sweetened coffee and soda throughout the day. Brushes once daily, does not floss or use interdental
aids. No prior periodontal therapy. **Blood pressure measured at today's visit: 186/114 mm Hg**;
patient is asymptomatic and says he ran out of his blood-pressure medication two weeks ago.

# Current Findings
- **Periodontal charting:** generalized probing depths of 5–7 mm in all posterior sextants;
  interdental clinical attachment loss up to 7 mm at the site of greatest loss. Bleeding on probing
  at ~45% of sites. Class II furcation involvement at #30; Class II mobility at #18 and #31.
- **Radiographs:** generalized horizontal bone loss extending into the middle third of the roots,
  with angular (vertical) defects mesial to #18 and #30. Generalized moderate-to-heavy subgingival
  calculus. One tooth (#31) has bone loss to the apical third and is questionable.
- **Caries/dental:** two cavitated coronal lesions and root-surface caries on #19 and #20; visibly
  reduced salivary pooling.
- Percentage bone loss relative to the patient's age is high, and destruction is out of proportion
  to the visible biofilm.
