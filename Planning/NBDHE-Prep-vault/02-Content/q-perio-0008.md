# Stem
A patient returns one week after periodontal flap surgery for suture and periodontal dressing
removal. Which home-care instruction is most appropriate for this visit?

# Options
- A) Resume gentle brushing with a soft brush in the surgical area and begin chlorhexidine rinses as directed (correct)
- B) Begin normal flossing and interdental brushing immediately in the surgical site
- C) Discontinue all oral hygiene in the surgical area until the 6-week reevaluation
- D) Use a firm-bristle brush with vigorous scrubbing to remove new plaque buildup

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Once sutures and dressing are removed, gentle mechanical cleaning with a soft brush can resume in
the surgical area, supplemented by chemotherapeutic plaque control (chlorhexidine) while tissue is
still fragile and interdental aids are not yet tolerated.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Flossing and interdental brushes are too traumatic to newly healing tissue at one week and are
  reintroduced later, once healing progresses.
- C) Prolonged avoidance of hygiene allows biofilm to accumulate and can compromise healing.
- D) Vigorous, firm-bristle brushing risks trauma to the healing surgical site.
