# Stem
When sequencing a dental hygiene treatment plan for a patient with active periodontal disease and
multiple carious lesions, all of the following are appropriately included in the disease-control
phase EXCEPT:

# Options
- A) scaling and root planing of all affected quadrants
- B) oral hygiene instruction and reinforcement
- C) placement of the definitive fixed prosthesis (correct)
- D) caries excavation and temporary restorations to arrest active decay

# Correct answer
C

# Rationale
The disease-control phase aims to eliminate active infection and stabilize the dentition:
periodontal debridement, oral hygiene instruction, and interim restorative care to arrest decay.
Definitive fixed prosthodontics belongs to the later restorative/maintenance phase, after tissue
response to disease control has been reevaluated.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Scaling and root planing is the core periodontal disease-control intervention.
- B) Reinforced oral hygiene instruction is essential to arrest and prevent further disease.
- D) Excavating active decay and placing temporary restorations stabilizes the dentition during
  disease control, before definitive restorative planning.
