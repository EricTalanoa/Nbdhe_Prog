# Stem
When scheduling and treating a patient with well-controlled type 2 diabetes, the dental hygienist
should generally:

# Options
- A) book a long afternoon appointment after the patient has skipped lunch
- B) book a shorter morning appointment and confirm the patient has eaten and taken medication (correct)
- C) withhold all care until an endocrinology clearance is obtained
- D) skip vital-sign measurement to reduce the patient's stress

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Shorter morning appointments (when endogenous cortisol is higher) and confirming a normal meal and
medication schedule reduce the risk of intra-appointment hypoglycemia, the most common diabetic
emergency in the dental chair.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Skipping a meal before a long appointment increases the risk of hypoglycemia.
- C) Well-controlled diabetes does not require medical clearance for routine hygiene care.
- D) Vital signs should still be recorded; omitting them removes useful monitoring information.
