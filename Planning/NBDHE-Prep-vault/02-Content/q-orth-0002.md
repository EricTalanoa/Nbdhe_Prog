# Stem
Which combination of home-care recommendations most directly targets this orthodontic patient's
caries risk?

# Options
- A) A daily fluoride regimen plus interdental aids (floss threaders or interdental brushes) and cutting the frequency of sports drinks and snacks (correct)
- B) Switching to a firm-bristled brush and brushing harder to scrub plaque off the brackets
- C) Rinsing with water after meals as the sole measure, with no change to fluoride or diet
- D) Discontinuing all flossing until the appliances are removed to avoid dislodging the wires

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
This patient's risk is driven by heavy plaque retention around fixed appliances plus **frequent**
fermentable-carbohydrate exposures (sports drinks, crackers), which repeatedly drop plaque pH. The
highest-yield home care attacks all three levers: **fluoride** to promote remineralization,
**interdental aids** (floss threaders, interdental brushes, or a water flosser) to remove plaque
the toothbrush can't reach around brackets and wires, and **reducing the frequency** of sugary/
acidic intake so the enamel has time to recover between exposures.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) A firm brush and aggressive scrubbing risk gingival trauma and abrasion without improving
  interproximal access; technique and adjunctive aids matter more than force.
- C) Water rinsing alone does not deliver fluoride or address the high sugar/acid frequency, so it
  leaves the two main drivers of demineralization untouched.
- D) Stopping interdental cleaning is the opposite of what's needed — orthodontic patients need
  **more** interdental care (with threaders/interdental brushes), not less; proper technique does
  not dislodge archwires.
