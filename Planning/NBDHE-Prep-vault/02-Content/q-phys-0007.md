# Stem
Which cranial nerve provides motor innervation to the muscles of mastication (masseter,
temporalis, and medial and lateral pterygoids)?

# Options
- A) Facial nerve (CN VII)
- B) Trigeminal nerve (CN V) (correct)
- C) Hypoglossal nerve (CN XII)
- D) Glossopharyngeal nerve (CN IX)

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
The mandibular division (V3) of the trigeminal nerve carries the motor fibers that supply all four
muscles of mastication, along with the mylohyoid, anterior belly of the digastric, tensor tympani,
and tensor veli palatini. Loss of V3 motor function produces weakness of jaw closure and deviation
of the mandible toward the affected side on opening.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) CN VII (facial) is purely motor to the muscles of facial expression (and stapedius); it does
  not innervate the muscles of mastication.
- C) CN XII (hypoglossal) is motor to the intrinsic and most extrinsic muscles of the tongue, not
  the jaw-closing/opening muscles.
- D) CN IX (glossopharyngeal) supplies the stylopharyngeus muscle, taste and general sensation to
  the posterior third of the tongue, and parotid secretomotor fibers — not mastication.
