# Stem
Which amide local anesthetic is distinctive in being partly hydrolyzed by plasma esterases, giving
it a shorter plasma half-life than the other amides?

# Options
- A) articaine (correct)
- B) lidocaine
- C) mepivacaine
- D) bupivacaine

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Articaine contains a thiophene ring and, unlike other amides, an additional ester side chain. That
ester is rapidly hydrolyzed by plasma cholinesterase, so a large fraction is inactivated in the
blood and its plasma half-life is only about 20-30 minutes, lowering the risk of systemic
accumulation.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Lidocaine is metabolized in the liver, not by plasma esterases.
- C) Mepivacaine is also hepatically metabolized.
- D) Bupivacaine is hepatically metabolized and is long-acting, the opposite of a short plasma
  half-life.
