# Stem
Which factor in this patient's history most increases her risk for progressive periodontal
attachment loss?

# Options
- A) poorly controlled type 2 diabetes (HbA1c 7.8%) (correct)
- B) a 15 pack-year smoking history that ended 5 years ago
- C) use of a manual toothbrush
- D) daily metformin use

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Poorly controlled diabetes impairs neutrophil function and collagen metabolism, amplifying the
host inflammatory response to plaque biofilm and increasing risk for periodontal attachment loss.
It is a well-established, bidirectional periodontal risk factor.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Smoking is a major periodontal risk factor, but risk declines substantially after cessation;
  a 5-year former smoker carries less current risk than her uncontrolled glycemic status.
- C) Manual toothbrush use is not itself a periodontal risk factor when technique is adequate.
- D) Metformin is a diabetes management medication; it is not a periodontal risk factor and,
  unlike some other drug classes, does not cause gingival overgrowth.
