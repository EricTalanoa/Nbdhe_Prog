# Stem
A study finds that patients who chew gum have less caries, but gum-chewers in the sample also
happened to visit the dentist more often for routine cleanings, which independently lowers caries
risk. This uncontrolled third factor is best described as a:

# Options
- A) confounding variable (correct)
- B) dependent variable
- C) null hypothesis
- D) placebo effect

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
A confounding variable is a factor, other than the exposure being studied, that is associated with
both the exposure (gum chewing) and the outcome (caries) and can distort the apparent relationship
between them. Here, dental-visit frequency independently affects caries risk and is unevenly
distributed between groups, so it confounds the gum-chewing/caries association unless controlled for
(e.g., through randomization, matching, or statistical adjustment).

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) The dependent variable is the outcome being measured (caries rate), not an extraneous factor
  distorting the relationship.
- C) The null hypothesis is a statistical statement of no effect/association; it does not describe an
  uncontrolled third variable.
- D) A placebo effect is a psychological/physiological response to receiving a treatment (even an
  inactive one), unrelated to an uncontrolled variable like visit frequency.
