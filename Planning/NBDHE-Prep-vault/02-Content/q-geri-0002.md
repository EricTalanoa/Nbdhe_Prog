# Stem
When individualizing caries-prevention instruction for this patient, the most appropriate
adaptation to her oral hygiene routine is to:

# Options
- A) recommend a power toothbrush with an enlarged handle to compensate for her reduced hand dexterity from arthritis (correct)
- B) instruct her to switch to hard candy sweetened with xylitol as her main strategy for both dry mouth and caries control
- C) tell her that once daily brushing quality declines, flossing should be discontinued entirely to simplify her routine
- D) recommend she rely solely on her partial denture's clasps to hold plaque-retentive areas clean

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Individualized education should adapt the mechanics of home care to the patient's actual
limitations. A power toothbrush with a built-up or enlarged handle requires less fine motor control
than a manual brush and can meaningfully improve plaque removal for a patient whose osteoarthritis
has reduced her hand dexterity — directly addressing the plaque accumulation noted in her exam.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Hard candy, even xylitol-sweetened, is not a substitute for a modified hygiene device; sugared
  hard candy specifically (as she currently uses) is actively contributing to her caries risk.
- C) Reduced brushing quality is a reason to adapt the technique or device, not to eliminate an
  interdental cleaning aid altogether — that would raise, not lower, her risk.
- D) Denture clasps do not clean tooth surfaces and are not a hygiene strategy; this ignores the
  actual problem.
