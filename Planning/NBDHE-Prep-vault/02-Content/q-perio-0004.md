# Stem
For this patient, the most appropriate initial prescribed therapy is:

# Options
- A) scaling and root planing with re-evaluation in 4-6 weeks (correct)
- B) immediate referral for osseous surgery
- C) full-mouth extraction and implant planning
- D) prophylaxis only, with no subgingival instrumentation

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Generalized moderate chronic periodontitis with subgingival calculus and 4-6 mm probing depths is
managed initially with nonsurgical periodontal therapy (scaling and root planing), followed by a
re-evaluation appointment to assess tissue response before considering further intervention.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Surgical referral is considered only if nonsurgical therapy fails to resolve inflammation and
  pocket depths at re-evaluation, not as a first step.
- C) Nothing in the findings (mobility, attachment loss severity) supports extraction at this
  stage; Class I mobility alone does not warrant tooth removal.
- D) A prophylaxis addresses supragingival deposits only and would not remove the subgingival
  calculus driving this patient's active periodontal disease.
