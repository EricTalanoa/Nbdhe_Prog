# Stem
A patient completed active periodontal therapy six months ago and has since maintained excellent
plaque control with stable probing depths and no bleeding on probing. When determining this
patient's supportive periodontal therapy (maintenance) interval, the most important factor is:

# Options
- A) the patient's age alone
- B) the individual's ongoing risk profile: current disease stability, plaque control, and systemic/behavioral risk factors (correct)
- C) a fixed six-month interval that applies to every patient regardless of history
- D) the number of teeth present, independent of periodontal status

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Maintenance intervals in supportive periodontal therapy are individualized, based on ongoing
reassessment of disease stability, plaque-control effectiveness, and risk factors (e.g., smoking,
diabetes control). A well-controlled, low-risk patient may extend beyond the traditional
three-to-four-month interval used for higher-risk patients, so risk-based reassessment — not a
one-size-fits-all rule — drives the recall schedule.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Age alone does not determine periodontal risk or maintenance frequency.
- C) A rigid universal interval ignores individual risk and disease stability, which is the actual
  basis for maintenance scheduling.
- D) Tooth count does not by itself indicate periodontal stability or risk.
