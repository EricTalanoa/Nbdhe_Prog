# Stem
The maximum recommended dose of an injectable local anesthetic is most appropriately calculated
from the patient's:

# Options
- A) body weight (correct)
- B) age in years
- C) height
- D) number of teeth to be treated

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Maximum recommended doses are weight-based (mg/kg), up to an absolute ceiling. Calculating from
body weight lets the clinician stay below the dose that risks systemic toxicity, which is
especially important for smaller (e.g., pediatric) patients.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Age can flag patients who need extra caution (very young or elderly), but the dose itself is
  computed from weight, not age.
- C) Height is not used to determine anesthetic dosing.
- D) The number of teeth treated relates to how much solution is used clinically, not to the
  patient's maximum safe systemic dose.
