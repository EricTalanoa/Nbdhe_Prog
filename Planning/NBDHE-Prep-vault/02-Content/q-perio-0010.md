# Stem
For a patient successfully treated for moderate chronic periodontitis, the typical periodontal
maintenance recall interval is:

# Options
- A) 3 to 4 months (correct)
- B) 6 to 12 months
- C) 2 weeks
- D) 5 years

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
A 3- to 4-month supportive periodontal maintenance interval is standard for treated periodontitis
patients, based on the time it takes pathogenic subgingival biofilm to reestablish and the need to
monitor for disease recurrence.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) A 6- to 12-month interval is appropriate for a periodontally healthy patient at low risk, not
  one previously treated for periodontitis.
- C) A 2-week interval is far more frequent than needed for a stable, maintained patient.
- D) A 5-year interval would allow undetected disease recurrence and significant attachment loss.
