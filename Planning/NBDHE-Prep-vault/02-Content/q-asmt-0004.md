# Stem
A white plaque on the oral mucosa that cannot be wiped off and has no identifiable local cause is
clinically termed:

# Options
- A) leukoplakia (correct)
- B) pseudomembranous candidiasis
- C) an aphthous ulcer
- D) a hematoma

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Leukoplakia is a clinical term for a white patch that cannot be rubbed off and cannot be attributed
to another diagnosable condition. Because a portion can be dysplastic or malignant, it is a
diagnosis of exclusion that warrants biopsy.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) The white pseudomembrane of candidiasis can be wiped off, leaving an erythematous base.
- C) An aphthous ulcer is a painful ulceration with a yellowish base and red halo, not a white
  plaque.
- D) A hematoma is a red-to-purple lesion from pooled blood, not a white plaque.
