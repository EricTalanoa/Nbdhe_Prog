# Stem
The primary mechanism by which topical fluoride reduces caries is:

# Options
- A) killing all cariogenic bacteria on contact
- B) promoting remineralization and forming acid-resistant fluorapatite (correct)
- C) mechanically sealing pits and fissures
- D) increasing the resting salivary flow rate

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Fluoride acts mainly topically: it drives remineralization of early enamel lesions and is
incorporated into the crystal lattice as fluorapatite, which is more resistant to acid dissolution.
At higher concentrations it also has a modest antibacterial and enzyme-inhibiting effect.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) At typical topical concentrations fluoride inhibits bacterial metabolism but does not kill all
  cariogenic bacteria.
- C) Mechanically sealing pits and fissures describes sealants, not fluoride.
- D) Fluoride does not work by raising salivary flow.
