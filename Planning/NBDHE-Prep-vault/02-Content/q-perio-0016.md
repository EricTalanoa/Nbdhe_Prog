# Stem
At reevaluation 6 weeks after scaling and root planing, a molar site still probes 6 mm with
bleeding on probing, while all other sites have resolved to 3 mm or less with no bleeding. The
most appropriate next step for this persistent site is:

# Options
- A) refer for evaluation for possible periodontal surgery or additional targeted therapy at that site (correct)
- B) discharge the patient to a routine 6-month recall with no further action
- C) repeat full-mouth scaling and root planing on all sites, including the resolved ones
- D) conclude that the site is stable because probing depth alone does not indicate active disease

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
A residual probing depth of 5 mm or more with bleeding on probing after nonsurgical therapy
indicates an area that did not respond adequately and remains at risk for further attachment loss.
Isolated non-responding sites like this one are the classic indication for referral to consider
surgical access or other targeted therapy, even when the rest of the mouth has responded well.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) A persistent deep, bleeding pocket is an active risk site; routine recall without addressing
  it risks continued disease progression.
- C) The other sites already responded to therapy; retreating them offers no benefit and doesn't
  address the one site that actually needs attention.
- D) A deep pocket combined with bleeding on probing is a recognized indicator of continued
  inflammation and risk, not a benign finding to ignore.
