# Front
Why does buffering a local anesthetic with sodium bicarbonate speed up its onset?

# Back
It raises the solution's pH closer to the drug's pKa, shifting more of the drug into its
uncharged (base) form. The uncharged form crosses the lipid nerve sheath more readily, so the
anesthetic reaches the nerve and takes effect faster (and the injection often stings less).
