# Stem
Immediately after removing an alginate impression from the mouth, the hygienist should rinse it
under running water and then:

# Options
- A) disinfect it with an EPA-registered intermediate-level disinfectant spray, following manufacturer contact time, before pouring the cast (correct)
- B) pour the cast in dental stone right away without any disinfection step
- C) let the impression air-dry on the countertop for several hours before disinfecting
- D) soak the impression in the disinfectant solution for at least 30 minutes

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Alginate impressions must be disinfected to break the chain of cross-contamination before they
leave the treatment area, typically with an appropriate spray-and-wrap technique for the
manufacturer-specified contact time, then rinsed again before pouring. Extended immersion is
avoided because alginate imbibes water and distorts.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Skipping disinfection risks transmitting pathogens to the lab/model area.
- C) Alginate begins to dehydrate ("syneresis") and distort within minutes of air exposure; a
  multi-hour delay ruins dimensional accuracy regardless of disinfection.
- D) Prolonged immersion causes alginate to imbibe water (imbibition), distorting the impression;
  brief spray disinfection is preferred over soaking.
