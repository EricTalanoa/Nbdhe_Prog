# Stem
A dental hygiene patient begins wheezing and reports shortness of breath during an acute asthma
exacerbation. In addition to administering the patient's own rescue inhaler, which positioning is
most appropriate?

# Options
- A) Supine, with the legs elevated above the level of the heart (Trendelenburg position)
- B) Upright or semi-reclined, whichever position the patient finds most comfortable for breathing (correct)
- C) Full supine with the head turned to one side
- D) Trendelenburg position, with oxygen withheld until EMS arrives

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
A conscious patient in acute respiratory distress (asthma, COPD exacerbation, CHF, or angina)
breathes easier upright or semi-reclined, since this reduces the work of breathing. Positioning
such a patient supine — appropriate for vasovagal syncope — can worsen dyspnea instead of helping.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Trendelenburg (supine, legs elevated) is correct for vasovagal syncope, to restore cerebral
  blood flow, but it is inappropriate here and can worsen a conscious patient's breathing
  difficulty.
- C) Turning the head to the side matters for airway protection in an unconscious or seizing
  patient, not for a conscious patient in respiratory distress who needs an upright posture.
- D) Supplemental oxygen should be administered (not withheld) to a patient in respiratory
  distress if trained and available; withholding it is inappropriate regardless of positioning.
