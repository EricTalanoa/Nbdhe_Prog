# Stem
One week after open flap debridement, a patient returns for suture removal and postoperative
evaluation. Which instruction is most appropriate for the hygienist to reinforce for the next two
weeks at the surgical site?

# Options
- A) resume normal brushing with a manual toothbrush and floss at the site immediately
- B) continue gentle plaque control with a soft brush and chlorhexidine rinse, avoiding vigorous mechanical cleaning directly at the site (correct)
- C) avoid all oral hygiene at the surgical site until the six-month reevaluation
- D) begin daily unsupervised subgingival instrumentation at home to keep the pocket clean

# Correct answer
B

# Rationale
Immediately after periodontal surgery, healing tissue is fragile; chemical plaque control
(chlorhexidine rinse) plus very gentle mechanical cleaning with a soft brush limits trauma to the
surgical site while still controlling biofilm elsewhere, until the tissue is healed enough to
resume routine mechanical hygiene at the site.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Vigorous mechanical cleaning right at a healing surgical site risks disrupting the wound and
  delaying healing.
- C) Stopping all hygiene for months would allow biofilm accumulation and threaten the surgical
  outcome; some form of plaque control must continue.
- D) Subgingival instrumentation is a professional procedure, not a home-care task, and is
  inappropriate this soon after surgery.
