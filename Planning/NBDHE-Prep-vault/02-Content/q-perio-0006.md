# Stem
The primary purpose of periodontal maintenance (supportive periodontal therapy) after active
treatment is to:

# Options
- A) monitor for recurrence and periodically disrupt the biofilm to control disease (correct)
- B) permanently cure the patient's periodontal disease
- C) replace the need for daily self-performed plaque control
- D) whiten the teeth at each recall visit

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Biofilm re-forms continuously, and periodontal susceptibility persists after therapy. Maintenance
visits (often every 3 months initially) re-evaluate probing depths and bleeding, remove new
deposits, and reinforce home care to prevent recurrence and detect progression early.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) Periodontitis is managed, not cured; the goal is stability, not a permanent cure.
- C) Maintenance supplements, and depends on, effective daily home care — it does not replace it.
- D) Whitening is unrelated to the goals of periodontal maintenance.
