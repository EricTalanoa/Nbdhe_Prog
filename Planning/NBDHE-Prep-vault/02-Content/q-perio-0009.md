# Stem
At the 4- to 6-week periodontal reevaluation following scaling and root planing, all of the
following are appropriate to assess EXCEPT:

# Options
- A) probing depths and bleeding on probing
- B) gingival tissue tone and color
- C) plaque and biofilm control
- D) radiographic evidence of alveolar bone height change (correct)

# Correct answer
D

# Rationale
Soft-tissue healing and reduced inflammation are evaluated at 4-6 weeks post-therapy because
connective tissue reattachment and reduced probing depths are measurable by then. Radiographic bone
remodeling occurs far more slowly and is not expected to show meaningful change on a 4- to 6-week
film, so it is not a useful reevaluation measure at this interval.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- A) Reduced probing depths and resolution of bleeding on probing are the primary clinical
  indicators of treatment response at reevaluation.
- B) Firmer, less erythematous tissue reflects resolving inflammation and is assessed at
  reevaluation.
- C) Reevaluating plaque control identifies whether the patient's home care is adequate to sustain
  the treatment result.
