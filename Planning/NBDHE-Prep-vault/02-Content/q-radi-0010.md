# Stem
A well-defined, round radiolucency at the apex of a nonvital tooth, continuous with a widened
periodontal ligament space, is most consistent with:

# Options
- A) a periapical granuloma or cyst (correct)
- B) a normal mental foramen superimposed over the root
- C) hypercementosis
- D) condensing osteitis

# Correct answer
A

# Rationale
Chronic apical inflammatory lesions (granuloma or radicular cyst) from pulpal necrosis classically
present as a well-circumscribed periapical radiolucency continuous with a widened PDL space at a
nonvital tooth's apex.

**Why the distractors are wrong**
- B) A mental foramen is a normal anatomic radiolucency, typically near mandibular premolar apices,
  not tied to a widened PDL space or pulpal nonvitality.
- C) Hypercementosis is an excess deposition of cementum, appearing radiopaque (a bulbous root
  outline), not radiolucent.
- D) Condensing osteitis is a localized radiopaque (sclerotic) reaction to chronic low-grade
  inflammation, not a radiolucency.
